Bali Tourism

Without a doubt, Bali is the Island of Gods.

The archipelago has gained quite a name for itself among tourists. Its popularity soared significantly after its paradisiacal portrayal in the novel Eat, Pray, Love.

Every other nook and corner of the island is nothing but a picturesque beauty waiting to be captured as an Instagram snap in the modern sense of it.

The tropical paradise has earned its reputation as one of the most beloved tourist spots in the world due to the various mystical attractions it holds within.
With its very own active volcano, multitude of Balinese Hindu pilgrimage sites, glorious beaches and waterfalls, Bali is basically a sacred ground aloof from the busy hustling life of metropolitan cities. Housing innumerable wildlife species, the place offers the best of everything.

Whether you want to enjoy a relaxed and laidback getaway or make your way to the numerous cultural landmarks as conventional tourists, Bali has it all for you.

Even the Indonesian actor Joe Taslim has quite a lot to say about his favorite Indonesian destination i.e. Bali - “I was there with my family in Nusa Dua, and my kids loved it. I’m a workaholic, so for me, Bali is a place where you can have a vacation, but you can have your own moment as well. You feel like you blend with nature – and I love the beach.’

The place has a magic of its own and the same can be felt through the embracing and welcoming gestures of the locals living there too.

It’s very difficult to stop oneself from praising the heavenly abode that is Bali. Therefore, it’s better if you take a trip down to the island yourself and be amazed by it all firsthand.

We’ve listed below every other intricate detail that you may need to get well acquainted with Bali before packing your bags for the adventure of a lifetime.

Enjoy your trip to the fullest!

How to Reach

By Air



Air travel is probably the best and most suitable option to reach Bali from international locations. If traveling from Mumbai, you can board Garuda Indonesia, a direct flight to Bali. Other flights flying to Bali are Malaysia Airlines, Etihad Airways, Singapore Air, Air Asia, etc. Air Asia has quite a few affordable options as well.

These flights usually have their pitstops either in Kuala Lumpur or Singapore, from where you can board the connecting flight destined to Bali. This is one of the common plans adopted by tourists since the two airports are well connected internationally.

Ngurah Rai International Airport, also known as Denpasar International Airport should be your final destination, marking your entry into the Indonesian province.

By Road



Traveling via road to Bali is not possible owing to its status as an island. However, if you still wish to add road travel to your journey, one way to do so is by opting for it to reach Singapore.
From there, your next stop will be Jakarta (via ferry), and then finally board a bus to Bali.
Covering this last bit of the journey will almost take up an entire day.

In any case, this option will weigh heavy on your wallet and is also very time-consuming. Hence, not an ideal mode of travel.

By Cruise



This alternative isn’t always viable but those interested in traveling via the sea route have an ample number of options as most cruise ships covering the South-East Asian regions stop at Bali.

Network Connectivity Status

The archipelago’s popularity has witnessed a drastic boom over the last few years, making it one of the top global tourism hubs. As a result, network availability has also improved to cater to a large incoming tourist population annually.

Cafes, restaurants, and hotels provide their own WiFis now and 4G connections are prevalent all over the island.

The primary triad of network providers on the island is XL, Smartfren, and Telkomsel. Faster access to the internet is possible majorly in the southern half of Bali.

Bali is a very pocket-friendly location so getting a pre-paid local SIM card will not be a hassle. Keep your ID proofs and passport handy to procure your SIM card, and it can be done at the airport itself.

As long as you’re not in an extremely secluded location, your network and internet facilities will be available easily. There are quite a few coworking spaces in Bali as well which provide good internet connections. If you head over to Canggu, the coworking space adjoining Zin Cafe should be your destination in case you need some work hours to yourself, surrounded by a good ambiance.

Top 5 Places to Eat

Queens of India



Starting off this list with an Indian eatery to cure your homesick blues in Bali.
Offering both vegetarian and non-vegetarian delicacies, Queens of India’s meat-based recipes do have an extra edge over other options on their menu. Their Harra Chicken Tikka is one luscious preparation that you must try if you’re making your way to this restaurant.

This authentic fine dining Indian restaurant chain is located in Kuta, Seminyak, Ubud, and Nusa Dua.

Locavore



To get a good dose of local Balinese food, you must try Ubud’s very own Locavore, boasting the motto “Go Local Or Go Home”.

Chefs Eelke Plasmeijer and Ray Adriansyah hold down the fort at the restaurant and make sure to prepare all their recipes using only local ingredients. The rice-based non-vegetarian dish “Into The Sawah” is one of their specialties.

You won’t regret experiencing the upscale dining scene here. The savory taste of food accompanied by its presentation and great service will be unlike anything you’ve ever seen before.

Coffee Cartel (Petitenget, Seminyak)



Seminyak’s brightly lit Coffee Cartel lures tourists to this abode of coffee. An invigorating cup of joe will lift your spirits like never before. On top of that, ordering Ripple Coffee allows you to have your own choice of detailed and intricate coffee art.

The service is fast. The food is delicious. The coffee is aromatic. What else could you ask for to enliven your mood?

Naughty Nuri’s



Found in 1995, Ubud’s Naughty Nuri’s is a top-notch restaurant chain in Bali that is famous for its martinis and barbecued pork ribs.

Just like the place’s name, the servers carry out naughty antics while pouring you a drink. It definitely drives the crowd’s attention towards your table but it’s all worth it in the end because you won’t witness a serving experience like this anywhere else.

BLANCO Par Mandif



Authentic Indonesian cuisine is served in a sophisticated exclusive setting that only allows 28 customers at a time. The BLANCO Rennaisance Museum Complex houses the restaurant which stands out among the other show-stoppers on this list. With a range of 7-9 food courses, BLANCO Par Mandif’s menu relies on the degustation option. Reservations are mandatory since only limited seating is possible.

The restaurateurs make it point to serve their dishes with panache that glorifies Indonesian heritage.

Top 5 Dishes You Must Try

Babi Guling



The undebatably most famous dish of Bali - the suckling pig. It’s nearly impossible to find this specific dish anywhere else but in Bali. Initially only served during religious festivities, it is now a common addition to the restaurant menus.

Mie Goreng



It’s one of the staple foods of Indonesia. A type of fried noodles that is paired up with chicken or pork and vegetables. Sometimes served with toppings like peanut sauce, fried egg, pickles, and chicken sate. The difference between Mie Goreng and Chowmein is that the former is made of egg noodles.

The beloved snack has been turned into the world-famous instant noodle pack labelled Indomie, a spicier analogue of Nestle Maggi.

Sate



One of the most popular and favourite street food of all time in Bali is Sate. It’s a quick snack made of barbecued chicken on skewers. Various categories of Sate include chicken, fish, beef, etc. Other than being prepared by street hawkers, it can also be consumed at restaurants.

Nasi Campur



A filling and mouthwatering plate served with mixed rice and loads of side dishes like tofu, meat or fish makes up for this distinct Balinese dish. It’s served all over Indonesia but the Balinese Nasi Campur is a unique concoction, all dues credited to Balinese spices.

Pisang Goreng



Finishing this list of must-haves is a dessert menu. Both street vendors and restaurants alike prepare their own renditions of this dish of fried bananas. People usually pair it up with vanilla ice cream to elevate their taste palette.

Local Culture and People

Balinese Hinduism i.e. Agama Hindu Dharma is the dominant religious faith believed in by the people here. It posits a focus on nature and the spirit, and the same is also reflected in the natural environs of Bali.

Around 20,000 temples can be found on the island. Each one of them has its own rituals and unique elements.

The people’s faith originates from the philosophy of Three Causes of Goodness i.e. ‘Tri Hita Karana’. It’s due to this belief that one can encounter a unique peaceful harmony in Bali as the people deify such a prosperous relationship among each other and with their God and nature.

One of the major festivals celebrated in Bali is the Nyepi and Balinese New Year in March which goes on for six days. This festival is a special celebration as it doesn’t revolve around all the pomp and show, rather is all about silence and all activities coming to a halt. Even tourists have to observe the following customs of fasting, meditating, etc as shops and beaches are closed off as well.

Another distinct celebration on the island is April’s Ubud Food Festival. The diverse cultures and cuisines of Indonesia are exhibited with pride during the span of three days. With live music performances accompanying visions of people demonstrating their cooking skills, this festival is bit of an extravaganza in itself.

Best Time to Visit

High tourist influx can be witnessed throughout the months of July, August, December and the first half of January. If you’re looking forward to a relaxed vacation, then these months may not be the right pick for you.

Visiting Bali during the off season in months from April to June, September and October may be a more appropriate option for you. During this time there’s no need to be anxious about coming across huge crowds on the island. Plus, it’s considerably dry season so you don’t have to fear heavy precipitation ruining your adventurous escapades either.

Since the place isn’t too congested during this time frame, enjoying water sports can easily be at the top of your to-do list and can be conquered hassle-free too.

Ultimately, it comes down to your preference of landing on the island either during the peak season or off-season.

Essentials You Need to Pack for This Trip

Depending on which season you’re traveling in, your list of essentials will vary accordingly. For example, Monsoon demands you to bring along waterproof gear, etc to protect your belongings.

Indonesian culture as a whole is very conservative. Therefore, you need to be wary of all the prevalent and in-action regulations regarding the same there. Although tourists are free to adorn clothing of their preference, the general guidelines remind people to wear clothing that covers both their knees and shoulders fully. This should be specially taken care of when entering a place of worship.

Remember to pack a pair of athletic clothes and suitable shoes for adventure sports like hiking. It’s better to have sunblock with you at all times, and a pair of comfortable sandals so that you can endure the heat.

Basic first-aid is absolutely necessary considering you’ll be engaging in several adventure activities.

Most importantly, documents like Identity Proofs, Passport, Visas, and photocopies are something you can’t leave your house without.

Top Places to Visit in the Vicinity

Kuta



In the southern half of the island, tourists find their beloved hotspot in the old fishing village i.e. Kuta. The beach-resort area is now a happening tourist development that is famous for its sunsets, water sports, accommodations, etc. It’s all about adventure, fun, and frolicking in the sun’s heat. The Kuta Beach, Kuta Theatre, Upside Down World Bali, are some experiences that attract tourists here other than it being a surfer’s delight.

Ubud



Located not too far from Bali’s capital Denpasar, Ubud is yet another popular place on the map worth visiting. Ubud is derived from the word Ubad which means medicine, and it falls in place as Ubud is well known as the healing center of Bali. The Sacred Monkey Forest, Lempuyang Temple, and Mount Batur are some of the majestic tourist spots here you can’t miss out on. Movie buffs may also recognize the place from “Eat, Pray, Love”.

Canggu



Just like Kuta, Canggu is also a village-turned beach resort. Villas and upcoming accommodations can be spotted among the terraced rice fields. The black sand Canggu Beach, the surfing hub Echo Beach and the picturesque Hindu temple Tanah Lot are some of its star-studded attractions.

Uluwatu



South Bali offers yet another surfer’s paradise in Uluwatu. One can peacefully explore this place away from the congested and overcrowded mainstream towns of Bali. A unique private beach experience awaits you at Thomas Beach with its sparkling azure waters; relish a mesmerizing sunset view at the Sunset Point Uluwatu Bar while sipping your favorite drink, or witness the legendary Kecak Fire Dance at Uluwatu Temple Complex and a lot more.

Umalas



A rather quiet tourist destination in Bali that packs the best experiences of all its surrounding locations. Umalas should be your choice if you want all of the best things together but in a more peaceful setting. Its close proximity to Seminyak, Canggu, and Petitenget allows you to travel to either place in the span of 15-20 minutes.

Seminyak



In stark contrast to its crowded counterpart Kuta, Seminyak is the gateway to a more peaceful vacation. Connecting chains of several restaurants and cafes situated on the coastline serving delectable delights are a luring sight. Diving into art at the Nyaman Gallery, Scuba Diving, Horse Riding, visiting Pura Petitenget, and Surfing at Seminyak Beach are the crowd-pleasing diversions here.

The Nusa Islands



Away from the bustling atmosphere of tourist hotspots, your escapade to the Nusa Islands is bound to be a calming affair. These are a group of three islands- Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida. Each counterpart of the trio has its distinct specialties. Nusa Ceningan houses a majestic blue lagoon; Nusa Lembongan has Dream Beach, Mushroom Bay where tourists can enjoy various water activities like riding on speedboats, snorkeling, kayaking, etc; Nusa Penida is the largest of the three, and its T-Rex Bay, bird sanctuary, and caves lure tourists to this island.

Denpasar



The provincial capital of the island is probably bound to be your first encounter with Bali. Being the largest city and the main hub of the province, Denpasar houses the international airport through which tourists can make their way to the island. Other than that, the city holds cultural significance as numerous temples dot its landscape. Some popular attractions located here include Shark Island, Bali Provincial Public Museum, Bajra Sandhi Monument, and more.

Jimbaran



Mostly known for its multitude of seafood restaurants, Jimbaran is a fishing village in Bali. Typically, it’s a highly sought-after tourist location in case you want the taste of a laidback and relaxing staycation. Kutu and Pecatu are adjoining nearby regions. You can embark on a short journey to these places if you’d like to encounter some cultural sites and monuments.

Lovina



Bali’s north coast enjoys the advantage of having the largest resort area in Lovina. Black volcanic sands cover the length of Lovina’s beaches and it stretches up to 8 km of coastline. Snorkeling and diving are the major activities tourists tend to enjoy here. In addition to that, the vision of bottle-nosed dolphins playing in the waters is a unique beauty you can’t afford to miss.

Top Attractions in the Vicinity

Mount Batur



If you wish to behold the vision of an active volcano on the island, Mount Batur is the place to be. You can hike up to the apex and sit at the top while marvelling at the scenic imagery around you. Witnessing a sunrise from this location is a heavenly experience. Other than that, you can take a dip in the hot springs near the volcano after your accomplishing your arduous hike to the top.

Pura Besakih



Bali’s very own largest temple complex is also known as Bali’s Mother Temple as it has been settled on the slopes of Mount Agung for the last 1000 years. It’s a marvel in itself as it still stands erect post the calamitous volcanic activity of 1963. The temple has carved a space for itself as a holy sanctuary in the midst of lush green rice paddies where the locals visit to offer their prayers.

Pura Ulun Danu Bratan



Located on the shores of Lake Bratan, Pura Ulun Danu Bratan (or for short Pura Bratan) was built during the 17th century. It’s also well known as the Floating Temple in Bali, and was constructed to pay tributes to Lords Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva. Its serene location and typical Balinese architecture make visiting the temple a worthwhile experience.

Bali Safari and Marine Park



Also known as Taman Safari Indonesia III, it is basically a safari park that was first opened to the public in 2007. Wildlife visions from Indonesia, Africa and India can be seen roaming the grounds of this park. Some of these include Bali mynahs, the Komodo Dragon and more. While being seated in your safari bus, you will get to catch a glimpse of these animals being swell in a natural setting unlike them being entrapped behind cages in zoos.

Mega show performances like that of “Bali Agung - The Legend of Balinese Goddesses" are yet another fascinating attraction you must experience while you’re here.

Tanah Lot



A picturesque off coast location holding historical significance to Balinese Hindus is a place of worship dedicated to the guardian sea deity. It’s also known as Khayangan Jagat among locals. A cave can be pictured in front of the temple, inhabited by a holy snake. Locals believe it to be protector of the temple.

In addition to the temple being situated on a huge rock, one can stay for the sunset views that are a majestic affair here.

Sekumpul Waterfall



Air Terjun Sekumpul or simply known as Sekumpul is the best waterfall in Bali. A total of 7 waterfalls make up the whole of Sekumpul. The cascade is hidden and fully enclosed in lush green environs, attributing an unworldly characteristic to it. The whole setting seems so surreal as if it were taken out of a CGI produced movie set.

Uluwatu Temple



Yet another Balinese Hindu Temple worth visiting here is Uluwatu Temple. Your visit to this temple will be a spiritually as well as culturally uplifting experiences. The breathtaking views atop the cliff of the temple inspire awe as one lays eyes upon the azure waters of the Indian Ocean. The sunset view from here is one to die for. To gain a bit more cultural insight, you can also watch the Kecak Fire Dance.

Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary



Home to the Balinese long-tailed Macaque, the sanctuary has many names including Ubud Monkey Forest or Mandala Suci Wenara Wana. A huge population of monkeys can be found here and since it’s their natural habitat, they feel comfortable enough to roam freely and pull all sorts of antics. Keeping your valuables handy may not turn out to be a favourable thing as they might they mind end up in the hands of the notorious monkeys. An ancient Hindu temple dating back to the 14th century can also be spotted nearby.

Lempuyang Temple (gates of heaven- 6 sanctuaries in the world)



It’s local name is Pura Luhur Lempuyang and is believed to be one of the most ancient structures of its type in Bali, which only adds to its sacred factor. The gates of the establishment that welcome public inside are commonly known as “The Gates of Heaven”. It forms part of the six main and holiest temples of Bali, collectively known as “Sad Kahyangan Jagad”, meaning “six places of God”.

Must-Do Activities You Can Enjoy in the Vicinity

Rafting Down the River Ayung



Ayung is the longest river in Bali with its river rafting trail stretching up to 10 km. Being surrounded by ricefields, waterfalls, and rainforests on each side will only elevate your experience of this adrenaline-driven water sport.

Tulamben’s Wreck Diving



Get ready to witness a one-of-a-kind shipwreck at Bali’s Tulamben. The remains of the US Army Transport Ship “liberty” lie here. You can dive deep into this sea adventure and imagine yourself being a part of your very own featurette of Pirates of the Caribbean.

Relieve All Your Stress and Tension at a Spa



Nothing can beat a soothing and rejuvenating day at the spa. Bali’s spas are a sight for sore eyes. They are the gateway to a paradisiacal time away from the incessantly racing life of one’s daily routine. Additionally, their location overlooks the glowing reflection of Bali’s natural beauty, which only heightens the spiritual healing process.

Surfing in Kuta



Kuta Beach is a quite famous spot for amateur surfers who are either just getting the hang of this activity or are beginning to get into it. The waves here are just the perfect height, without introducing any unprecedented dangers.

The Mount Batur Hike



Often claimed to be a rather easy hike, this trail hasn’t always proved to be an easy path for everyone. However, once you reach the top, you’ll agree that it was worth all the toil and hard work. The sunrise view from this active volcano site is unbelievable and rewarding.
