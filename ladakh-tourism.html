Ladakh Tourism

“Although I deeply love oceans, deserts and other wild landscapes, it is only mountains that beckon me with that sort of painful magnetic pull to walk deeper and deeper into their beauty. They keep me continuously wanting to know more, feel more, see more.”

                                                                                                                               - Victoria Erickson

Probably one of the best places to visit in order to come face to face with the proper amalgamation of cultural heritage and nature’s grandeur is Ladakh.

The place is a rugged cold desert where temperatures drop to an unbelievable extent. It touches World’s highest saltwater lake i.e. Pangong lake. One can bear witness to a great load of picturesque beauties here that are hard to find elsewhere, like, snow leopards, the double-humped camel, confluence of several rivers, a glorious view of lofty ice-capped mountains. The list goes on.

One of the most common takeaways from Ladakh is the Chadar trek, where adventure enthusiasts get to tread over a frozen lake, taking challenges to a whole new level.

Beyond these sublime spectacles, the people living here are openly welcoming and take pride in the beautiful festivals they organize over the year. The Hemis festival is one such occurrence that commemorates the tenth day of the Tibetan Lunar calendar. During this time, it’s as if the whole town’s adorned with colorful embellishments and happiness. It’s a sight for sore eyes that rejuvenates one’s mind and body altogether.

Upon visiting monasteries, one’s serenity is peaked owing to the tranquil air that runs through these gigantic architectures decorated with intricate works of art on their walls.

The overall experience felt throughout one’s stay at this haven of flora and fauna, cultural diversity and nature is a spiritually enlightening engagement with a pocketful of adventure on the side.

How to Reach Ladakh

Depending on your choice of transportation, you can choose from either of the three to reach Ladakh.

Make sure to consider the weather conditions at the time of your trip before coming to any conclusions.

By Train



Ladakh doesn’t have a train station so there’s no direct train destined to Ladakh. Situated 700 km away from Ladakh is the Jammu Jammu Tawi train station which is the nearest station. You can easily travel directly to this station via trains from Mumbai, Delhi, and Kolkatta. Thereafter, you will have either have to commute via a cab or a JKSRTC bus to travel from Jammu to Ladakh.

By Flight



This is probably the most ideal method of transportation to reach Ladakh. Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport is the closest airport situated in Leh at a mere distance of 3.8 km from the city. Flights from Delhi, Chandigarh, Jammu, Srinagar, and other Indian locations easily connect with this airport.

By Road



The first route that leads to Ladakh is from Manali, Himachal Pradesh, which is 494 km away. The other route, at a distance of 434 km, is through Srinagar.

You also need to keep in mind that the highways are closed during the Winters because of heavy snowfall and slippery roads. So, you can’t travel to Ladakh during this season via road.

Otherwise, the cab or jeep facility or a JKSRTC bus that you can board from Kargil will lead you to your snow-clad destination.

Network Connectivity Status

Owing to its location and militarized position, prepaid SIM cards are non-functional in Ladakh, so you must avail a postpaid connection. Similarly, foreign SIM cards won’t work here either. You need to purchase a local prepaid SIM. For this procedure, you may be asked to show your identity proof.

If one were to ask about the best mobile network in Ladakh, then it’s definitely BSNL. It covers a wide range and its internet connection is great in Leh but starts to get weaker as farther you get from there.
Airtel is yet another network operator that works wonders in Leh with its high-speed 4G internet. However, depending on your location the connection can get weaker. For example, in Diskit, Hunder, Leh-Kargil road, your network will transform into 2G or 3G connectivity.

Jio is a rather new network but its 4G connectivity gives rise to high-speed internet in Leh and its surrounding areas. Nubra Valley, Diskit, and Hunder also experience the same.

Some remote villages of Ladakh are cut off from such networks and so the Digital Satellite Public Telephone (DSPT) has been installed there for emergency purposes. They charge Rs. 2 per minute but can only be used in cases of emergency and aren’t meant for one’s personal use.

Top 5 Places to Eat

Bon Appetit



Facing the picturesque view of mountains, Bon Appetit is a multi-cuisine restaurant located south of Changspa Road. The indoor seating plan is just as warm and homely, decked with books and board games.

This restaurant is the ultimate package as it offers the best of food and ambiance. You can enjoy rich servings of wine and beer here in the midst of nature along with your loved ones.

One can easily say that the eatery’s pizzas take the crown in the whole menu. Moreover, the chocolate momos are an intriguing addition to bring one’s meal to a savory closure.

The Tibetan Kitchen



Serving a delicious dose of Tibetan, Chinese and Tandoori cuisines, the Tibetan Kitchen is one of the most famous joints in Leh, found on Fort road.

There are enough options available on the menu to satisfy both vegetarians and non-vegetarians, nevertheless, the chicken thupka has been an all-time favorite dish among the customers.

Regardless of the availability of other dishes, you must try their authentic Tibetan food. It’s an extraordinary experience that foodies will relish.

Alchi’s Kitchen



This restaurant rose to fame due to its authentic Ladakhi food. Food is served here in its traditional preparation or as a modern hybrid of sorts. Either way, it’s something your taste gland will come to appreciate.

The specialties of this eatery are Tashi Tyage Tea, the Khambir (bread), and Chutagi (pasta-like dish). Owing to the availability of such food options, the restaurant is also able to cater to the tourist's desire of wanting to have a taste of the local experience. The interior is also decorated in such a style that you’ll feel at home in this Ladakhi setting.

Namza Cafe



Whether you want to indulge in your food cravings or the desire to shop, you can have a hand at both of them simultaneously at the Namza cafe which is a boutique/cafe. This cafe not only grants your wish of buying high-end traditional Ladakhi attires but also has a bite of those olden-era recipes that can easily pass as antiquities.

Situated at Zangsti road, this restaurant offers warmth to its customers with its must-have dishes, Yarkandi Pulao.

La Piazzetta



On Changspa road, this one-of-a-kind restaurant that lines up dishes across multiple cuisines like Ladakhi, Tandoori, Kashmiri, European, etc is built.

There are three different seating areas- with cushions on low seats, indoors or outdoors. The overall ambiance of the place is starstudded with wall murals. Plus, bonfires on extremely cold days with an impeccable outside view of the majestic nature make this restaurant the ideal hangout spot to visit with one’s friends.

Top 5 Dishes You Must Taste

Momos



These vegetable or meat dumplings served either steamed or fried are the most famous dishes of Ladakh. Locals treat their relatives with momos on the Ladakhi new year, Losar festival.

Owing to their ever-rising popularity, chocolate momos are a new addition to the experimental palette introduced by some restaurants in Ladakh.

Thukpa



Thukpa is easily the most consumed staple food of Ladakh. Noodles made of wheat are served in vegetable soup. It’s a very healthy dish and is regularly served with chutney to spice its rather simple taste. The broth is beneficial in warming one up especially during a typical Ladakh Winter.

Chutagi



The literal translation of “chutagi” is “water bread”. This famous Ladakhi dish looks like pasta and every local restaurant has its own special ingredient to bring a new twist to it. It basically consists of small dough pieces in the shape of bow-ties that are cooked along with a vegetable soup. It’s commonly served during the harvest season since it’s a very heavy and nutritious meal.

Skyu



Skyu is a traditional soup dish containing vegetables and small dough balls. It’s often paired up with meat but even in itself, it’s a pretty heavy meal and quite filling. Ladakhi people relish this delicacy mostly during Winter as temperatures tend to drop severely low. Sometimes milk is also added to the recipe. The dish is then called “oma sky”, with oma meaning milk.

Tingmo



Thus steamed Tibetan bread is like a bun that is often served with dal, meat, or cooked veggies. It’s a staple Ladakhi food that is eaten all throughout the three meals of the day or even as a tea-time snack.

Local Culture and People

The foremost aspect that one notices about Ladakh’s culture is that people follow the Buddhist and Tibetan tenets and customs. The people living here are believed to be very humble and down to earth and are closely connected to their customs. They follow the traditional code of conduct in their lives and the same gets reflected in the way they celebrate their festivals.

In the people’s demeanor, one can feel their deep connection with their religion, especially the Buddhists. They believe lamas to be the channel of communication between common folk and god.

Their union is perhaps best felt during the harvest season when everyone comes together to cultivate the land. Monasteries are at the center of their lifestyle and tradition and also act as a ground for annual celebrations.

Languages spoken here include Ladakhi, Tibetan, Urdu, and Balti. The most commonly spoken languages by the locals are Tibetan and Ladakhi.

The original make-up of the population comprised of the Indo-Aryan race known as Dards. However, now one comes across more people of Tibetan descent. Other than that, a community of Muslims called Arghons also resides in Leh.

Ladakhi people are always welcoming and their warm-heartedness hits home.

Best Time to Visit

Ladakh experiences extremely harsh climatic conditions and low temperatures during Winter. Therefore, it’s best to plan your trip to Ladakh during the summer season when the temperatures vary from 15 to 30 ℃ on average, making it the ideal condition for tourists to go about their itinerary without anything pulling them back.

For this, you’re looking at a window from April to July to book your tickets to Ladakh if you don’t want to be at the mercy of the cold weather and rather enjoy a delightful time during the summers.

On the other hand, tourists who seek to witness the cold fury of Ladakh’s winter wonderland also tend to visit the place during the winter season. This gives one the opportunity to be a part of the Frozen River Trek aka Chadar Trek, one of the most challenging and difficult treks in the world.

If your aim is to plan the trip while keeping Chadar Trek as your foremost priority, then the timeline you need to be following is from mid-January to March.

List of Essentials You Need for This Trip

Before listing all pieces of clothing and other essentials you need for this trip, you need to divert your attention to the fact that you’ll be in a mountainous terrain which can easily lead to motion or altitude sickness. Therefore, bringing medicines for such situations is extremely vital.

Thereafter, you must remember to carry important documents like your Voter ID, driving license, or Aadhar card as proof of your identity. Visiting certain locations in Ladakh isn’t an easy passing affair. You need to have permits to enter some places as they are considered as the Protected areas, like Pangong Tso Lake. Indian citizens are required to have Inner Line Permit (ILP), whereas foreign nationals must have a Protected Areas Permit (PAP).

Temperatures drop lower than one can anticipate so you need to prepare for the unexpected beforehand. Carry multiple thermal layers of clothing to protect yourself from the harsh cold climate. Nevertheless, the layers you carry along should be comfortable enough to allow swift movement for activities like trekking. Keep your woolens including gloves, caps, etc handy. Wear properly insulated shoes to counter the cold.

A simple emergency first aid kit containing antiseptics, pain relief ointments, bandaids, and medicines for common maladies like headaches, allergies, etc should be with you at all times.

You must also have some cash on you since ATMs aren’t as easily accessible in the mountain area.
Finally, it’s a remarkable journey and one needs to film it to capture all the memories. Get your cameras and phones ready with extra phone chargers and power banks as a backup.

Top Places to Explore in the Vicinity

Alchi



The Alchi village is located on the banks of the Indus River and it is a great hit among tourists since several monasteries dot the map of this village. The place is about 3500 m above sea level.

Colorful festivals held here further attract the attention of tourists like the Hemis festival in June and the Ladakh festival that goes on for the span of 15 days on an annual basis.

Nubra Valley



This valley is one of the best sites you need to visit when in Ladakh. White-sand deserts, camels, Karakoram range, Shyak, and Siachen rivers, all of them co-exist at the same place and add to its charm. The “valley of flowers” (Dumbra - Tibetan name) is also known as the “Orchard of Ladakh”.

Turtuk



Bordering both India and Pakistan, this village allows you to steal a glance of Mt K2. Nearing the end of the village, you can also find a Buddhist monastery. From here, one can enjoy the panoramic views encompassing the whole valley.

Explorers may also visit the small Baltic museum established by the succeeding family of the Baltic rulers, that too free of cost. If legends and folklore excite you, then you must not let the opportunity of visiting this place slip by at any cost.

Khardung-La Pass



At a height of 5359 m, Khardung-La is a famous motorable road pass in India. It is the second-highest pass after Dungri La. People interested in motorbiking or other adventure activities often find their way to this location. It also leads tourists to the glorious visions of the Karakoram range and Nubra and Shyok valleys.

Magnetic Hill



A board here announces that this place experiences “The phenomenon that defies gravity…” The Magnetic Hill is surrounded by a magnetic field, and so when vehicles pass by, they might feel the effects of the same. If you wish to witness this Bermuda triangle-like effect in India itself, you should consider visiting this place.

Zanskar Valley



Home to the worldwide famous Zanskar river aka the frozen river, Zanskar valley is a snow paradise situated at an altitude of a distance of 13154 ft. It’s the one place you need to be if adventure sports are your thing. Trekking, paragliding, rafting, etc can all be tried out at this one place. Furthermore, historical sites like various monasteries can also be explored here.

Dah and Hanu



These villages are located 163 km from Leh. The Inner Line Permit (ILP) is necessary to facilitate mobility to these villages. People who admire exploring local cultures and their diversity are in for a treat. You will come across the last Aryan race here, and its people have done their best to stay rooted in their traditions. It’s quite amazing how they’ve managed to counter the modern, metropolitan ways of life and adhered to their olden ways of lifestyle.

Hunder Village



Hunder is a sequestered village making it a likely location for adventure activities like camping, trekking, or camel safari.
At an altitude of 10,000 ft, Hunder is the confluence of the Shyok and Nubra rivers. In order to reach here, you have to travel through the second-highest motorable road pass aka Khurdang La. Another attractive affair that draws tourists to this place is the presence of the Bactrian or double-humped camel. It’s the only place in India where they’re found.

Top Attractions in the Vicinity

Lamayuru Monastery



It’s one of the largest and oldest Tibetan and Buddhist monasteries in Leh. Situated at a height of 3510 m. The soil around this monastery is similar to what one would supposedly find on Moon, being all rugged and uneven, and so it’s also known as the “Moonland of Ladakh”. One can find here beautiful and unique stupas and rocks with prayers carved into them. This historical landmark dates back to the 10th century and carries the heritage of the Drikung Kagyu tradition to this date.

Pangong Tso Lake



This location may have shot to fame due to its cameo in the Bollywood blockbuster 3 Idiots but it has always been a sweet spot for a warm getaway with one’s friends or family. You can set up camp here and relish the serene Himalayan airs, away from all metropolitan troubles.

Takthok Monastery



This is yet another Buddhist monastery located in Ladakh’s Sakti village. This is the only standing monastery that seeks to propagate the Nying-ma-pa or the Red Hat sect of Tibetan Buddhism. Commonly referred to as Thak Thak or Thag Thag by the locals, the monastery is a heritage site dotted with elegant temples decorated with wall murals and paintings of Lord Buddha.

Shanti Stupa



On one of Chanspa’s hilltops, Shanti Stupa’s white beauty glistens and attracts tourists. From this spot, one can glance over the entirety of Leh from a high vantage point. The interior of the dome-shaped Chorten displays the imagery of Lord Buddha’s life trajectory. As the name suggests, and due to its location, the place offers a chance to be at peace i.e “Shanti” in the presence of the bounteous nature that surrounds the dome structure.

Tso Moriri Lake



At about 15000ft above sea level, right in the midst of rocky and rough environs is the Tso Moriri Lake. The presence of a pristine and clear water body at such a high altitude is astonishing in itself. Such a location makes way for a peaceful and calming atmosphere. Some consider it to be a paradise on earth, all thanks to the quiet and undisturbed setting located away from the bustling cities.

Royal Leh Palace



Situated on the Tsemo hilltop, the Royal Leh Palace is a 9-stories building made of wood, stones, and sand. The vintage and ruinous architecture of this attraction is what draws people to it as it embodies the past of the lives led by the royals. Another name for the place is Lhachen Palkhar and it used to be the residence of the Namgyal dynasty during the 17th century.

Hemis National Park



Located in the high altitudes of northern Himalayas, Hemis National Park is the Snow leopard capital of India. The park occupies an area of 3350 sq km which makes it the largest National park in India. Other than snow leopards, you can also spot the rare Bharal here. The park got its name from the Hemis Gompa monastery, situated near Leh. You can’t miss out on coming here if you want to encounter some rare species of flora and fauna.

Chadar Trek



Having to be able to walk over a frozen river is once in a lifetime kind of opportunity. Chadar trek grants that wish for you as you get to walk on the frozen Zanskar river while being surrounded by the snow-decked Himalayas. After the trek, tourists can camp at Nerak to complete the whole adventure sports package.

Castle of Sani



This is one of the most prominent historical attractions of Leh Ladakh that was built with the blessings of Oddhyiyana’s Padmasambhava. The castle has the cemetery of Dechendal and the Stupa of Kanishka inside it. Due to the presence of a meditation cave inside as well, it is believed that Guru Kanishka stayed and meditated here for quite a long period.

Stok Palace



Displaying the splendors of the royal family’s heritage, the Stok palace is home to various antique royal paraphernalia like crowns, etc. The place was built by King Tsepal Namgyal in 1820 and is now open as a public exhibit to tourists after being blessed by the holy Dalai Lama.
The place has beautiful gardens that will take your breath away at the first glance itself.

Top Activities You Can Enjoy in the Vicinity

Yak Safari



Yaks are naturally accustomed to the rocky terrain of Ladakh and so traveling across Ladakh’s landscape on their backs is an unexpected joyride that you must give a try at least once. The journey will bring you closer to other tranquil aspects of the place like crystal water lakes and the sublime mountains. Completing the course of the beautiful and serene environs of a valley with a Yak is unlike any experience you might have had to date.

Skiing



Owing to its constantly cold climatic conditions throughout the year, Ladakh is one of the best places to get a taste of this sporting activity. The Khardung La range provides an ideal spot for ski enthusiasts to try their hand at this thrilling adventure. Various skiing competitions are also organized during the Winters, and they are a sight to behold.

Cycling



Indulging in this activity in Ladakh can be a bit of an arduous task owing to its rugged and uneven terrain. You can change your course for the same depending on your preference of difficulty level. Cycling on straight roads will grant you the opportunity to halt at pit stops and make conversation with the locals of the area. Regardless of which course you may choose, the surrounding natural beauty of Ladakh will accompany you at all times.

Some irresistible destinations you can cycle to are Khardun La Pass, Pangong Tso Lake, Tso Moriri Lake, Namgyal Tsemo Gompa, etc.

Trekking



Some of the best treks in Ladakh are Chadar trek, Darcha Padum trek, Dran Drung Glacier trek. The harsh terrain of the place sets the path for the most challenging and rigorous trek experiences you will experience throughout your entire life. Yet, accomplishing this feat is very self-rewarding and the adrenaline rush one gets during this activity is worth all the effort.

River Rafting (Indus River)



“Singhe Khababs”, meaning “out of Lion’s mouth” is another name for the Indus River. Adventure enthusiasts will find themselves in the arms of nature while rafting through the Indus River.
There are different courses to choose from depending on their difficulty level. Beginners or less experienced rafters can opt for a more quiet and calm route that begins from Hemis and ends at Choglamsar. A more challenging route on the other hand is the one that stretches between Alchi and Khaltsey.

Camel Safari (Nubra Valley)



Enjoying the scenic views of Ladakh while traveling on the back of a camel is an extremely gratifying experience. What makes it even more interesting is the fact that Ladakh is the only place where you can find the double-humped camel or Bactrian camel in India. Even the prominent deserts of India lack this spectacle.
