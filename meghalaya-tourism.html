Meghalaya Tourism

“Adopt the pace of nature. Her secret is patience” - Ralph Waldo Emerson

Nature’s organic presence is amiss in our modern existence. And so, to fuel one’s desires of being content and at peace in the serene embrace of nature, we choose to embark on getaways to mystical lands that appease our hearts.

Rich with biodiversity, Meghalaya, known as the “Abode of Clouds”, is one such place that completely pulls you towards it. Owing to its irresistible charms and beauty, you have no choice but to give in and fall for its scenic enchantment that transports you to a magical space away from the urban metropolitan commotion.

To plan your itinerary for Meghalaya, you need to have some basic idea about what to expect from the state’s grand range of attractions that will leave you mesmerized.

We’ve broken down each and every aspect that must be taken into count by tourists while planning their next trip.

How to Reach Meghalaya

By Train



The nearest railway station to Meghalaya is in Guwahati, situated around 100 km away from Shillong. This station becomes the connecting ground for other state travelers going to Meghalaya since the state itself doesn’t have any railway station of its own.

By Flight



The only airport in Meghalaya is at Umroi, a town located 35 km from Shillong. However, this airport isn’t well connected to other states owing to only Air India regional flights being functional here.

Therefore, the other alternative, in this case, is Guwahati’s Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport, 128 km away from Shillong. Tourists often hire cabs from the airport to complete their journey to Meghalaya. Otherwise, another affordable connecting means of transport is the helicopter service that can take passengers aboard from Guwahati to Shillong in a short span of 20 minutes itself. However, this service isn’t available on Sundays, so if you’re thinking of going for it then plan accordingly.

By Road



Assam State Transport Corporation (ASTC) and Meghalaya Transport Corporation (MTC) buses can be used to commute between Guwahati and Shillong. The comfort level of the buses varies as per your budget. You can choose between a range of sleeper, luxury, and Volvo buses.

NH40 connects Guwahati to Shillong so one can even take a long drive covering 135 km, covered over a period of 3 to 4 hours. If you’re choosing to drive yourself, then your drive’s onset location will be Guwahati’s Bara Bazaar that will take you to NH37. From here, Kamrup district will link you to NH40.

Traveling by road has its risks considering how rainy the state is but this route is the safest of them all and will get you to your desired destination in no time.

Network Connectivity Status

Indoor connectivity is a bit of a hassle considering the weather conditions that dominate in Meghalaya and due to numerous forests being dispersed over the state landscape.

Jio and Airtel offer considerably better services than others. Despite BSNL being one of the oldest network service providers, it doesn't work as it used to some time ago.

People have complained against poor data connectivity even after 4G connections have been established.

Thereafter, a 4G Base Transceiver Station (BTS) site was inaugurated in November 2021 which sought to improve data connection and voice communication for the people of the village Ri Bhoi. This move on the part of the government was also initiated to promote tourism.

Top 5 Dishes You Must Taste

Delicacies of a state embody its tastefulness and specialty. Of course, you can find faux alternatives anywhere but the dishes specific to a particular place will be served authentically only in their original home ground.

The preparation of the same dish can vary depending on which area you’re in, considering all three main tribes in Meghalaya prepare their food differently. Their staple ingredients include rice and meat, however, vegetarian alternatives are also available.

Here’s a list of the top 5 dishes you simply can’t afford to miss out on.

Jadoh



This is one of the foremost traditional Khasi dishes and in their language the name is a combination of ‘Ja’ meaning rice and ‘Doh’ meaning meat.

It can be considered a usual bowl of biryani, however, the addition of different kinds of meat and spices is what sets it apart. And so, it’s nowhere similar to an ordinary serving of biryani.

Pumaloi



The versatility of rice allows it to be eaten with any kind of food combination of one’s choice. Pumaloi is another prominent Meghalayan dish whose preparation revolves around rice. It’s bound to make an appearance during the festivities of Meghalaya. Its preparation requires one of a kind pot called Khiew Ranei.

Nakham Bitchi



The locals in Meghalaya welcome their guests by serving them this soup. Its base ingredients include dry fish and vegetables. It’s a healthy item on the menu that is usually eaten after spicy meals.

Doh-Khlieh



An easy-to-prepare recipe with a minimal requirement of four ingredients, Doh-Khlieh is yet another pork recipe of the Khasis.

Momos



Now, most of you will be thinking as to why momos have been included in this list especially since they can be found all over India. They’re deemed to be one of the interesting spicy street foods most youngsters opt for.

Nevertheless, once you try the Meghalayan preparation of momos, you will be able to acknowledge the authentic taste of momos, that you may not have experienced anywhere else.
North Indian momos tend to serve a Punjabi rendition of the same, in addition to many more flavours that have been included in the palette.

Nevertheless, momos in Meghalaya are a delicious treat you can’t ignore. They’re served hot, either fried or boiled as per your preference.

Top 5 Places to Eat

The most prominent eateries and cafes of Meghalaya can be found in the state capital Shillong. A wide range of eateries dots the map of Shillong. You can visit them as per your food preferences. Whether you’d like to try out local food items on the menu or simple multi-cuisine dishes if you’re feeling homesick, all can be found there easily.

Cafe Shillong



This cafe is one such name that can be found in most planner guides for Meghalaya. It has one of the most dashing interiors and is bound to impress music enthusiasts without a doubt.

Located at Laitumkhrah, Cafe Shillong has both in-house and outdoor seating plans. The overall ambiance and service are very sophisticated and the menu serves you both an ample number of vegetarian and non-vegetarian options. Nevertheless, you might want to try Shillong noodles and Khao Suey as these two form the star-studded specialties of the cafe.

Jadoh



If you’re trying to go easy on your wallet while still wanting to enjoy authentic Khasi dishes, then this is the place for you.

Jadoh is one of the cheapest yet classiest eateries in Shillong and offers you vegetarian alternatives for pork and beef in Khasi delicacies. The service is quite quick and won’t make you doubt your decision to eat here.

As the restaurant’s name suggests, Jadoh is the evergreen specialty offered. The place exudes warmth and close knitted-ness and is an ideal eating outlet for students owing to its budget-friendly services.

Dylan’s Cafe



Ever heard of cafes having a fanbase? Well, Dylan’s Cafe definitely has one. The cafe’s interior decor pays homage to music legend Bob Dylan and sets the mood right with mellow lights.

It’s almost like an arcade that allows customers to add their works of art onto the tiles on the roof. Moreover, you can have a great time with your friends while playing board games, swaying to the music, and even going shopping for cute mementos.

Desserts like Mississippi Mud Pie will enliven your mood. Both vegetarian and non-vegetarian options are available. Although the place is a bit heavy on one’s budget, yet you’re bound to have a gala once there.

Phunga Manipuri Restaurant



This is yet another restaurant that grants your wish of digging into authentic local food. The interior also sets a similar mood owing to the bamboo decorations and other local paraphernalia that reminds one of Manipur.

The menu offers a vast range of dishes from Manipur, Assam, Arunachal, and even Khasi specialties.

It’s a simple restaurant without any boisterous elements interrupting the calm. Therefore, you can peacefully indulge in the delicious treats along with lovely company by your side.

Bread Cafe



Established a decade ago, Bread Cafe forms an integral part of Shillong’s landscape.
Once you enter this eatery, you just need to kick back and relish the mouth-watering delights, especially the pastries.

You must visit this cafe if you want to enjoy some light snacks. The cheque also amounts to an average level when considering it from the budget aspect.

Cheesecake, black forest cake, Fudgy Coffee, Mousse, and Cheese croissant are some bestsellers that might catch your eye.

Local Culture and People You’ll Meet

The Abode of Clouds is indeed a multicultural and diverse abode to three dominant communities, namely Khasis, Garos and Jaintias. Each community has its own identity and inhabits the respective areas as follows- The Garos in the western area, the Khasis in the central and the Jaintias in the eastern.

Each of these ethnic communities majorly follows the matrilineal system despite their other specific traditional differences. People here are mostly followers of Christianity. Otherwise, temples, gurudwaras, mosques and monasteries can also be spotted in Meghalaya.

Their festivities are a flamboyant celebration of pomp and fervor. Each community has its own set of major festivals. The Khasis celebrate a five-day-long festival called KaPemblangNongrem dance annually. Whereas, Jaintias celebrate their most significant festival Behdiengkhlam in July at Jowai. Finally, the Wangla festival is held for over a week to pay tributes to the Sun God of the Garos during October-November.

And so, folk tradition unites the people of Meghalaya, which is also why the state is home to so many traditional dance forms.

The official language of the state is English, and other dominant languages include Khasi and Garo. Many can even be seen interacting in Nepali and a few other languages.
Regardless of their individual identity, the residents of Meghalaya are joyful, warm and quite welcoming.

Best Time to Visit

Each season has its own merits to offer, and so it’s a very subjective choice as to which side of Meghalaya you’d like to discover.

The best time to visit the place depends on your itinerary. Whether you want to indulge in adventurous activities or just enjoy a laidback getaway and be in the moment, will be the final determining factor for this riddle.

Meghalaya’s rains have a mystic aura, thereby resulting in Mawysnram’s acquirement of the moniker, “the wettest place on Earth”. If you wish to envision this sight firsthand while staying aloof from the bustling crowd, then this is the most appropriate time for you to plan a trip to Meghalaya.

Conversely, the April-June month span encompasses the entirety of the Summer season. This is a rather comfortable season in terms of how it allows tourists to head out and explore various locations freely, unlike the Monsoon. It’s during this period that you can meet all kinds of crowds in the area due to the celebration of numerous festivals like the Dance of Contentment. This rather colorful time of the year makes way for tourists to conquer endless adventures while trekking or hiking more than any other.

Essentials You Must Pack For This Trip

During the Summer season, the temperature rises and falls between the range of 16 to 31 degrees celsius. Therefore, it’s better that you carry both warm and cool pieces of clothing. Sunscreens, hats, and sunglasses may be some other accessories that can come in handy.

Rainy season brings the dangers of stagnant water, so one must bring along mosquito repellents and appropriate pair of shoes. You can’t do without umbrellas and raincoats during this time. Even otherwise, you can be met with unexpected rains during other months too. To be on the safer side, just tuck in your raincoats regardless of the season. It’s also advisable to bring along some extra clothes just in case you end up getting drenched in the rains.

Winters on the other hand tend to get considerably cold even though not exactly unbearable. Thermal wear or fleece clothing should keep you warm enough and action-ready for hiking and other activities.

Heading out for adventure demands extra precautions so a medical kit is a sure-shot necessity. Additionally, keeping some ready-to-eat snack options in one’s proximity never hurt anyone.

Tents, sleeping bags, proper trekking shoes, and flashlights form part of the adventure kit essentials without a doubt.

Finally, our lives are governed by technology and Meghalaya is one such place whose memories you’d like to revisit from time to time. Therefore, clear up your phone storage or get your camera to capture as much as possible. Extra batteries and power banks will come to your aid when traveling to remote areas.

Top Places to Explore in the Vicinity

Mawlynnong Village



In 2003, this village won the title of the cleanest village in Asia. The environs surrounding this village are proof of why the village bagged this title. Other than that, it’s also called “God’s Own Garden” owing to it paradise-like beauty.

It’s a gateway to numerous natural attractions like the Mawlynnong waterfall, root bridges, and wilderness that set it in stark construct with the congested view of metropolitan cities.

Cherrapunjee



Except for Mawsynram, Cherrapunjee also constitutes one of the wettest places. The place has a lot of locations to offer tourists as a means of escape.

Nohkalikai Waterfalls



1115 ft tall, Nohkalikai is the tallest plunge waterfall in India. A local legend is associated with the waterfalls, which makes the visit even more meaningful. The location boasts of an entirely natural existence that hasn’t been marred by man-made inventions.

Don Bosco Museum



If you’re one of those history fanatics who loves to dig into a place’s cultural and historical relevance, then you must visit Don Bosco Museum. The museum boasts of the cultural heritage of the state and is one of the largest museums in Asia.

7 floors and 17 galleries of the museum put out a cultural fest for tourists who are interested in finding out more about the lives of the indigenous people.

Khasi Hills



This is yet another location that gives the tourists a chance to detox from their engaging everyday lives and to rejoice in the midst of delightful and heartwarming natural environs.

It’s quite famous among the tourists since a lot of other places are found here like Umiam lake, Mawlynnong and Dawki river.

Police Bazaar



Stepping from sublime beauty for a while, Police Bazaar is the one vision of commercial life (especially at night) in Meghalaya where you can find all kinds of dining places and have a good meal after indulging in intense activities like trekking etc.

The bazaar complex also has numerous shops from where you can get your dose of authentic traditional local handicrafts and souvenirs.

Laitlum Canyon



Found 21 km from Shillong, is one such landscape that hasn’t been explored by many tourists, yet the picturesque view offered by the place is something that your cameras won’t be able to capture or justify.

A staircase even allows you to descend down 3000 steps and carry on with your wanderlust spirit.

Balpakram National Park



Located near the Garo hills, about 134 km from Shillong, this national park is 3000 ft above ground level. It covers 200 sq km of land, where one can find diverse flora and fauna flourishing in a healthy natural ecosystem. One can catch a glimpse of Deer, hillock gibbons, leopard, and golden cat here.

Local myths also call this place the “Land of Spirits”.

Elephant Waterfalls



This three-tier waterfall is the closest association with Meghalaya most tourists have. It’s found right in the midst of a dense forest and almost feels like a magical land one finds in fairy tales.

Situated 12 km away from Shillong, this is one such location that you can’t afford to not have on your itinerary when visiting Meghalaya.

Mawplang Sacred Forest



One of the most sacred tropical rainforests of Meghalaya, this forest has housed a variety of trees and plants over the past 1000 years. The locals of the area regard the forest with a lot of respect as they believe it to be the home of a deity, Labasa, the protector of the people.

Top Attractions in the Vicinity

There are a lot of places one can venture off to in Meghalaya.

Among those locations, one may come across numerous heritage sites that allow us to take a glance at the place’s history, and transport the visiting tourists back in time.

Don Bosco Square



As the name of the place suggests, the biggest landmark of this attraction is the Don Bosco statue right at the center of the square.

One can call this a hangout spot for youngsters as the prominent schools and colleges of the city can be found here. It’s a great place to get a glimpse at the local lifestyle and enjoy authentic local food as well.

Megalithic Bridge on the Um-Nyakanah



Tall stone pillars and stone slabs support the foundation of the megalithic bridge across the Um-Nyakanah river.

It’s a heritage site as it was sanctioned during the regime of the Jaintia king after his move from Sutnga to Nartiang. This bridge was used as a channel for transportation between the two places.

Tourists can also find the Muwi falls in the vicinity.

David Scott Memorial Stone



This is a commemorative monument built to honor the East India Company officer during colonial rule.

The David Scott trail is rather famous among trekkers and extends up to 16 km and will take you up to the East Khasi hills.

Kiang Nongbah Monument



Situated alongside river Myntdu, the monument was built to memorialize the Jaintia patriot named U Kiang Nongbah who died in his fight against Britishers.

The location is close to nature and tourists often visit the place to pay their tributes to the martyr hero.

Khasi Heritage Village



This attraction is located 27 km away from Shillong. The village showcases a pictorial representation of the Khasi tribes and their ways of living and the cultural practices followed by them.

Some houses are deliberately built in the traditional Khasi design, and a museum can also be found in the area where tourists can come face to face with the kind of paraphernalia and tools used by the people.

An archery range also puts out the traditional bow and arrows of the tribe, which appeals to the tourists’ interest and curiosity.

Narthiang Monoliths



This Meghalayan collection of monoliths is rather the tallest one in the world. They act as a historical reminder of the Jaintia Kings’ reign. Erected around the period between 1500 AD and 1835 AD, these structures are situated 65 km away from the state capital.

Moo Iong Syiem has been noted to be the tallest monolith of all with a height of 8 m. It was erected as a memento by U Mar Phalyngki, of the Jaintia king’s victory in battle.

Stone Memorial of U-Maw Thodur-Briew at Nartiang



It is yet another set of monoliths that have been classified into menhirs, domens, and cairn types. These monoliths have been established to preserve the memory of the local tribe known as U-Maw Thodur-Briew. Out of all the megalithic stones used to build these, menhirs are usually the largest in size.

Must-Do Activities

Meghalaya offers you a chance to dive into its bounteous landscape while engaging in enthralling activities that you just can’t afford to miss.

Trek to the Double Decker Living Root Bridge at Cherrapunjee



Trekking to regular locales is quite common. However, once you trek to the living root bridges of Meghalaya, your entire idea about this activity will be transformed forever.

This is an experience unlike any other that will make you feel as if you’re part of the Meghalayan tradition since these bridges are a result of man-made efforts put in by the Khasi tribe. They’re a wondrous marvel situated amidst the dense tropical forest that makes you feel one with nature.

Water Adventures at Umiam Lake, Shillong



The lake covers over 10 sq km of the area. Locally known as Barapani, the lake is home to adventure sports like kayaking, skiing, water scooting, boating and more.

You can enjoy a tranquil time off your busy work schedule and take in the natural views around you while boating. It is also the most famous activity of all in Meghalaya since people across ages can go boating.

Biking at Sohliya Village



If you’d like to take your thrill fever a notch higher than just trekking in the natural terrain of Meghalaya, biking will feed your soul with immense joy.

While biking you will come across the various hill ranges and other scenic views. Sohliya village will allow you to escape into the countryside overflowing with bountiful nature and bring your loser to your humane side as you interact more with the locals in the area.

Camping Near Umiam Lake



Umiam Lake is a popular picnic spot in Meghalaya for water sports. The lake is surrounded by the lush beauty of hills and huge trees. The serene surroundings make iit a perfect spot for camping.

Shopping at Bara Bazaar Shillong



Meghalayan markets are replete with colors and transport you to a cinematic vision.

This is the one place where you can get your authentic Meghalayan mementos from. The market is flooded with local artworks, textiles and handicrafts.

River Canyoning at Sohra.



This activity requires the basic fitness of adventure enthusiasts since it involves a combination of numerous vigorous exercises having to do with trekking, mountain rappelling, and swimming altogether.

You start by trekking to the point where water bodies are situated, then rappel down the waterfalls and finally canyoning in water and swimming across boulders.

Guides will be there to assist you. Nevertheless, it requires a lot of effort, so you should consider your health conditions before taking the leap.

Golfing at Gleneagles



Owing to its scenic landscapes, Shillong has one of the best golf courses surrounded by pine groves and rhododendron trees.

The relatively new adjustments to the golf course have rendered playing the sport quite challenging. It adds to the reasons why tourists have a knack for engaging in the sport especially at Shillong Golf Course.

Exploring Mawsmai Cave at Cherrapunjee



These mesmerizing caves are a big attraction in Meghalaya especially since they’re the only caves that allow adventure seekers to view the natural stalagmite and stalactite rock structures inside. Tourists have access to the 150 m long area only even though the caves extend quite further ahead.

The variously shaped rock structures have been a result of the years-long presence of underground water, and erosion. This is a guaranteed thrill ride for those who seek mysterious activities to uncover.

Conclusion

One thing is for sure, your visit to Meghalaya will be a delightful affair. After having gone through this expansive and detailed explanation of all things related to the place, you will reach there as an informed and well aware tourist.

This will put you at ease during your trip since everything will be orderly planned by you beforehand, relieving you from any kind of anxiety that revolves around being in a new place altogether.

Happy and Safe travels!
