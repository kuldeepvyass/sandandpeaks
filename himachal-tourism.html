Himachal Tourism

“You’re of to Great Places! Today is your day! Your mountain is waiting, so get on your way!”

                                                                                                                                               - Dr. Seuss

The mountains are a great place to not only discover hidden gems and lesser known secluded locations but also oneself. Himachal Pradesh is replete with snow-capped places and lush valleys equally. Tourists get a chance to not only dive straight into its picturesque terrain but to also seize the opportunity and make the most of the thrilling adventure sports and endless possibilities that can be actualized here.

Even the name “Himachal” is formed by the coming together of “Hima” meaning snow and “Anchal” meaning lap, and quite literally, Himachal is located in the lap of snow i.e. the Himalayas. Its capital city is commonly referred to as the “Queen of hills”. Moreover, Dharamshala is recognized as the state’s second and winter capital.

The state’s landscape is dotted by Cedar forests and in turn, the Indian Cedar is acknowledged as the tree symbol of Himachal Pradesh.

It also houses about 33 Wildlife sanctuaries and 2 National Parks.

All in all, it’s a delightful place you need to witness first hand because pictures don’t do it justice no matter how high the resolution of the camera is. The authenticity of its beauty can’t nearly be captured in its entirety.

So, the next time you start planning a trip away from your busy schedules, just consider Himachal Pradesh as your destination and you won’t be disappointed.

To help you put together any itinerary, we’ve come up with all the things you need to know about Himachal below.

How to Reach

By Train



The two major broad gauge train stations that lead to Himachal Pradesh are Pathankot and Kalka.

From Pathankot station, you can travel to Jogindernagar and then connect you to the Kangra Valley. Whereas, the Kalka-Shimla track, which is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site will connect you to Shimla.

By Flight



Small scale airports of Gaggal (Kangra), Jubbarhatti (Shimla) and Bhuntar (Kullu) make air travel possible to Himachal. In addition to these, the Pathankot and Chandigarh airports bordering Himachal are also easily accessible.

If traveling to Dharamshala, Hamirpur, Chamba, Una, or Kangra, the Pathankot and Gaggal airports will lead you to your destination.
The Bhuntar airport is the right choice for places like Kullu-Manali, Lahaul-Spiti, and Mandi. Finally, the Jubbarhatti and Chandigarh airports will help you with conveyance to areas like Shimla, Bilaspur, Solan, Kinnaur, and Mandi.

By Road



Traveling via road routes from Pathankot, Chandigarh, and Kalka is quite easy. You can drive yourself or avail the public bus or taxi services to reach here. Other surrounding states like Delhi, Jaipur, Chandigarh etc have their interstate bus services as well.

Network Connectivity Status

The high altitude of Himachal often hampers network connectivity, which is why many remote locations within the state are deprived of internet connection. With passing years, connectivity has definitely improved but Himachal still hasn’t reached the ideal state.

Lahaul-Spiti Valley and Kinnaur don’t have enough network towers. As per the present situation, BSNL and Jio network work the best and offer better coverage than other network providers at such remote and distant locations.

Airtel, Idea and Vodaphone work in most areas but their connectivity is lost as soon one nears rivers. During such situations, 4G connections tend to fall down to the speed of 3G or lower, and at times even lose complete connectivity.

Tata Docomo and Aircel are not favourable network providers. Therefore, would recommend the travelers to switch their SIMs before leaving.

In any case, you need to prepared for intermittent network connectivity while traveling to Himachal, sometimes even for no network at all. Contact your family and friends to convey the same to them beforehand.

Top 5 Places to Eat

Oberoi Cecil Hotel (Shimla)



The restaurant’s interior design flaunts the chic ambiance of the old forgotten days by incorporating the fashions of a ballroom. Its impeccable style is topped off with lavish food servings. Some must-have specialties include Kullu Trout fish with lemon butter sauce, Fried fish, biryani and more. Both vegetarian and non-vegetarian options are available for order.

Johnson’s Bar and Restaurant (Manali)



Located in Siyal, this is the most famous restaurant in Manali that specializes in Indian, Italian and Continental dishes. A huge lawn adjoins the restaurant building facing the majestic view of mountains. The best delicacies prepared by them include Madra-palak, Manali trout-fillet curry with rice, apple crumble, Irish coffee etc. The main Manali market is within walking distance from here.

Evergreen (Kasol)



Israeli and Italian cuisines served at the Evergreen Cafe have to be its magnum opus. The kind staff members of the cafe will make you feel welcomed and serve you with finesse. Prices are budget-friendly and manageable. The mushroom soup is a delectable addition to their already mouth-watering food options. Beverages like beer, cold coffee, fresh juices, and ginger tea add to the full-flavored food experience.

La Plage (Kullu)



With outdoor seating in the midst of apple orchards, La Plage boasts of an extravagant wine collection. The total bill may be a bit heavy to bear if you’re on a tight budget. Nevertheless, the savory richness of the food quality justifies it. Like most Pahari food, La Plage serves meat and potato dishes in heavy amounts.

Lung-Ta (McLeod Ganj)



If you’d like to try appetizing Japanese cuisine while keeping your budget in check, Lung-Ta should be your go-to choice. The eatery has an additional warm inner seating arrangement along with one on the open terrace, allowing visitors to admire the spectacle of towering mountains while enjoying their meal. The restaurant is also affiliated with a non-profit initiative whose donations are given to Tibet welfare.

Top 5 Dishes You Must Taste

Madra



Emerging from the Chamba district, Madra is the epitome of the food culture followed in Himachal. It brings people together during various festivities and ceremonies. The dish is prepared in oil by seasoning spices with soaked chickpeas.

Tudkiya Bhath



Another delicacy coming from Chamba, Tudkiya Bhath is a rice-based dish. For its preparation, pulao is cooked with masoor dal, spices, quite a few veggies, and yogurt. It’s a traditional Pahari recipe. Adding some lemon juice and garlic will take its savory flavor to the next level.

Dhaam



This dish is famous in Chamba and Manali. Special chefs called “botis” prepare this traditional festive delicacy of Himachal Pradesh. Originally, it used to be served on leaves. Dhaam is typically a platter consisting of various lentil-based dishes. The individual foods that make up this platter are - rice, kadhi, madra, Mahni, meetha bhat, sepu badi, and more as per the region the thali is being served in.

Babru



The Pahari analog of Kachoris is stuffed with black gram filling and the resulting kneaded dough or flatbread is called Babru. It’s a crispy delight that is often served with tamarind chutney and Chana Madra. This healthy addition to one’s food palette is one of the staple dishes of Shimla.

Chha Gosht



Finally, a non-vegetarian heritage dish of Himachal Pradesh. For this, marinated lamb is uniquely prepared with gram flour, spices, and yogurt. A delectable gravy then covers the meat, adding more spice to the overall taste of the dish.

Local People and Culture

Hindi is the official language of Himachal but people tend to speak the Pahari dialect more. Other commonly spoken languages include Punjabi, Kangri, Kinnauri and Dogri.

After all this time, the people of Himachal have retained their cultural heritage and traditions despite the rapidly changing world around them. The place boasts of a diverse makeup where people follow various religions, Hinduism and Buddhism being at the forefront.

The various communities constituting the Pahari society are Rathis, Rajputs, Brahmins, Kannets, Kinnars, Pangawals, Lahaulis, Gaddhis.

The extravagant and pompous festivals celebrated here invoke feelings of community building. They easily become occasions where people can share their respective traditional recipes with each other as food acts as a common ground for expressing their affection.

Some prominent festivities of Himachal are the Shivaratri Fair, Mani Mahesh Chhatri Yatra, Chhat, Navratri, Kullu Dussehra, Renuka Fair etc.

Himachal Pradesh is majorly an agrarian society. Therefore, the locals are quite driven and hardworking, and at the same time humble. They understand the workings of society and its complex structuring. It’s a great pleasure to engage in any kind of interaction with them.

Best Time to Visit

The peak season is usually from March to June due to the pleasant and assimilating weather conditions which allow tourists to indulge in adventure sports like trekking etc. The summer season is also time for notable Pahari festivals which give way to events like the Hadimba Devi Fair, also known as Doongri Fair. These colorful festivities and occasions make up for a lively and fairly gratifying visit to Himachal Pradesh.

Although July to September is observed to be the low season, it’s during this time that the monsoon makes its way to Himachal and breathes new fantastical air into the lush green environs of the place.

The time period from December to February marks the Winter season. Due to the prevailing cold conditions during this period, people don’t often visit Himachal at this time. So, going to the hills during this time frame can help you dissociate from your usual hectic and hammering daily schedule as crowds tend to stay aloof.

Ultimately your decision hinges on your personal preference. You can go through this seasonal breakdown of the situation in Himachal and make up your mind accordingly.

List of Essentials You Need to Pack

Carrying your necessary ID proofs like Aadhar card, Voter ID proof, or others (some places may even require permits for traveling), and a printed copy of your itinerary has to be your utmost priority.

Depending on the specific location you’re planning to visit, your clothing essentials will also vary. Some regions like Solan, Bilaspur, Kangra, Una, and Hamirpur tend to get hot during the Summer season. However, others like Kullu, Shimla, Spiti, Chamba, and Kinnaur remain pleasant with chilly winds blowing during the day too.
You must pack your clothes accordingly and bring along full sleeve t-shirts and some extra layers for Summer, in case you’re not used to cold winds. Winters can be severe, so you’ll be needing good quality woolen clothes to protect you from the extreme conditions.

Visiting heavy snowfall areas requires you to bring along your gumboots for merely walking on the road. They can also be rented locally.

It’s better to travel with enough precautionary necessities beforehand. A basic medical kit containing first-aid like antiseptics, pain relief ointments or sprays, band-aids, sanitizers, and personal medication for altitude sickness, fever, body ache, etc is something you should have in portable bags.
The sun can often be too bright due to the high altitude. Therefore, sunscreen lotion needs to be packed for the trip as well.

Some eatables, proper shoes, toiletries, in-hand cash, camera, power banks, charger, etc are some obvious items that are usually forgotten.
Make sure to list your essentials before packing them so that nothing is left behind.

Top Places to Explore in the Vicinity

Shimla



To this date, the capital city of Himachal Pradesh has retained its colonial architecture including the Town Hall, Christ Church, Viceregal Lodge, and Gaiety Theatre. It has only the best of views to offer with its panoramic landscapes. At an altitude of 2205 m, the city is located at the foothills of the Himalayas which allows the tourists to witness its snow-clad visage during the Winter other than the abundant and fertile green topography.

Manali



At an elevation of 2050 m, situated on the River Beas valley, Manali turns into a white wonderland during the Winter season. Its landscape is dotted with pine and deodar forests, white mountains, and other natural beauties that have a stronghold on tourists. Following the conventional hill-station framework, Manali houses quite a few Tibetan monasteries and temples. Additionally, it’s a great spot for diving into adventure sports as well.

Kasol



This little village on the hills has a heavenly aura owing to its location on the banks of the Parvati River. Trekkers and backpackers take extra delight in visiting this place, often surrounded by the burble of the flowing river. Kasol has gradually climbed up the list of most popular hill stations to explore over the years, and rightly so. Endless rows of cafes serve finger-licking Israeli food here, and just the overall calming atmosphere of the place speaks for itself.

Kasauli



Perched at a height of 1927 m, Kasauli is a relatively small town located on the southwestern side of Himachal. It’s yet another place that displays its colonial heritage with attractions like the Baptist Church and Christ Church. Other crowd-pleasing landmarks include Monkey Point, Nahri Temple, Kasauli Brewery, Central Research Institute, and Kasauli Club. People who prefer a laidback vacation just for the sake of detoxifying themself from the turbulent bustle of cities tend to come here more.

Dharamshala



Also known as “Little Lhasa”, Dharamshala is a well-known and frequently visited tourist location in India. The place is also home to the Dalai Lama and so it’s a cultural and spiritual hub of Tibetan Buddhism. Being here allows one to immerse in interaction with multicultured communities, which in turn becomes an added attraction for foodies because multicuisine foods are served in ample amounts across chains of restaurants and cafes here. India’s “Mini Tibet” i.e McLeod Ganj, Sindhbari, Dharamkot, are some explorable landmarks.

Bir-Billing



There’s no way you haven’t heard of the “Paragliding Capital of India”. Trekkers and backpackers often head out to this place to step aside from their hectic daily schedules and fulfill their dream of experiencing a life-changing feat. Although adventure sports are the major aspect that pulls tourists here, the scenic views of the lush green landscape offer a relaxing and soothing outlook towards life.

Spiti Valley



Located on the borders of both India and Tibet, Spiti, the “middle land” is a rugged cold desert mountain valley that attracts adventure enthusiasts as much as it does spiritual travelers. Numerous Buddhist monasteries dot the map of this valley. Moreover, nestled at a height of 3810 m, this place is a great hotspot for a plethora of challenging sports like trekking, whitewater rafting, mountain biking, Yak safari, etc.

Barot



This is an extraordinary village that came about in the 1920s during the Shanan Hydel Project. Located in the Mandi district, it’s now become a prominent tourist location. Cedar forests cover the entire topography of this place and it’s well known for Trout fish farming. Trek routes headed to Kullu and Kangra valley also start from here. It’s one of the most serene locales can set out to explore.

Solan



With its other names like “City of Red Gold” and the “Mushroom City of India”, Solan is the third-largest city in Himachal Pradesh following Shimla and Dharamshala. Connoisseurs of Architecture and History especially will be enraptured by the set of ancient buildings that can be found in Solan, as there are umpteen numbers of ancient churches, temples, and monasteries here.

Chail



Surrounded by the greenery of the lower Himalayas, Chail is the best place to be if you love being embraced by nature’s bounty. Chail Palace, built by the Maharaja of Patiala in 1891, is one of the grandest royal landmarks you’ll come across. The palace has now been turned into a hotel and offers a cozy and comfortable ambiance. Furthermore, the Chail Sanctuary Wildlife refuge is at a distance of 26 km from there. Chail is also home to the world’s highest cricket ground.

Top Attractions in the Vicinity

Kangra Fort (Dharamshala)



This is one of the largest forts in India that used to be a part of the Trigarta Kingdom and was even referenced in Mahabharata, making it one of the oldest as well. The fort has been through a lot during this historical period and two temples also reside within - Ambika Mata Temple and Laxmi Narayan Temple.

Hadimba Temple (Dhungiri Van Vihar, Manali)



The other names of the temple are Hidimba Temple or Dhungari Temple. It was named after Hadimba Devi, whom you must know from The Mahabharata. An excert of the Hindu epic is also displayed for the people visiting the temple, narrating the account of Pandyas’ exile.

Nako Lake (Nako Village, Kinnaur)



At an altitude of 12,000 ft, Nako Lake is in great proximity to the Indo-China border. It’s oval shaped and attracts tourists from all corners. Summers make way for the recreational activity of boating in the lake. On the other hand, once the lake freezes in Winters, it becomes an ideal ground for ice skating.

Rashtrapati Niwas (Observatory Hill, Shimla)



The Viceregal Lodge or Rashtrapati Niwas is a historical landmark that was designed by Henry Irwin. It embodies the nation’s history during the colonial period as it used to be the residence of the Viceroy of India. Now, in the contemporary times, the place exhibits all the historical artefacts of the time including old documents.

Tabo Monastery (Tabo Village, Spiti Valley)



Being one of the oldest Buddhist monasteries in Himachal, Tabo Monastery is a predominant pilgrimage site for Tibetan Buddhists. It displays the Buddhist religion and its divine teachings at its best as quite a few of them can also been seen on the monastery walls. What’s unique about this monastery is that its settled below the valley instead of being at the top, and that makes room for a cave above it where monks can meditate.

Prashar Lake (Mandi)



With its close association with the legend of Mahabharata, Parashar lake is a highly sought-after tourist attraction in Himachal Pradesh. A three-storied temple structure bearing a resemblance to the Buddhist Pagoda stands right next to the lake. The location is enveloped by the panoramic view of mountains. The small floating island spotted in this lake is mobile and keeps shifting from its prior position throughout the year.

Rock Cut Temple (Othra, Beas Valley)



Also known as Masrur temple, this establishment dates back to the 8th century. The temple was founded to pay tributes to the major Hindu deities - Vishnu, Devi, Shiva and Saura. Prone to frequent earthquakes, the temple suffered through many damages. Nevertheless, the overall architecture and interior design of the temple is unique and unlike conventional temples out there.

Lord Elgin’s Tomb (McLeod Ganj, Dharamshala)



Don’t let your imagination amok but this tomb’s location is a bit off-beat amidst the dense Deodar forests. The tomb was erected to pay tributes to the 8th Earl of Elgin - James Bruce. Mass is also organized at aa place nearby. Although the visual may seem like one taken out of a dark movie, the place is a source of serene tranquility with the lush green forest embracing you.

Chandratal Lake (Spiti Valley)



Tso Chigma or Chandratal is located near the Chandra River, and it too has its association with the Hindu mythology. The surroundin environs of the lake are are quite rugged owing to the topography of the cold desert. However, it’s a sight for sore eyes during the Summer season, being covered with a variety of flora and fauna.

Naggar Castle (Kullu)



It’s a medieval castle built around 1460 AD by Raja Sidh Singh. However, in 1978, Himachal Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation converted it into a heritage hotel. Its high elevation allows the tourists to view the extensive Kullu Valley. The castle is basically a museum of sorts now and holds relics dating back to a long forgotten past and its traditions that existed at the time.

Activities You Can Enjoy in the Vicinity

Riverside Camping



Camping grants you a chance to give in to a laidback adventure that transports you mentally since you can finally have some time for yourself, away from daily chores. Some of the best camping sites in Himachal Pradesh are Beas Kund, Rasol (Parvati Valley), Grahan (Parvati Valley), Kheerganga (Kasol), Kutla Glacier (near Tosh), and more.

Trekking



There are countless trekking routes in Himachal. The Kheer Ganga Trek (Kasol), Jakhu Temple Trek (Shimla), Hampta Pass Trek (Pir Panjal), Triund Trek (Dharamshala), Pin Parvati Pass Trek, Chamba Trek, Great Himalayan National Park Trek, Tirthan Valley Trek, Deo Tibba Base Camp Trek, etc are some of the most famous treks that grant tourists with a life-changing experience as they get to witness the breathtaking landscape of the valleys around and the sublimity of snow-clad mountains. They all vary in terms of their level of difficulty. Therefore, trekkers can choose which one suits them as per their comfort.

Paragliding



Located in the Joginder Nagar Valley, Bir Billing is one of the most popular sites for paragliding in the world. The altitude difference between Billing (take-off site) and Bir (landing site) is around 800 m. No other adventure sport provides you with the opportunity to gaze at Himachal’s wondrous natural topography as paragliding does.

River Rafting



Rivers originating due to Himalayan glaciers make way for a daunting sport of white water rafting. The rapid currents of water result in an unbelievable instance of adrenaline rush driving the rafters. Some of these rafting attractions are River Sutlej (Tattapani) near Shimla, Ravi River near Chamba, Pirdi - Beas river near Kullu. Other challenging currents are that of the Spiti and Chandra rivers (Lahaul-Spiti).

Experience the Tibetan Opera



The annual Tibetan Opera Festival i.e. Shoton (meaning Yogurt) festival is celebrated at the Tibetan Institute of Performing Arts (TIPA) in McLeodganj to put on an exhibition of Tibetan heritage (Ache Lhamo). This artistic expression is merely a means of consolidating the Tibetan identity that has always been endangered by corrupt politics.

Go Skiing



The slopes of Himachal serve as a great ground for this winter sport. Some of the ideal skiing destinations of the state are located in Kufri, Narkanda, Manali, Solang Valley, Rohtang Pass, Gulaba, Hanuman Tibba.

Explore and Pay Respects at Monasteries



Himachal Pradesh is highly influenced by the Tibetan culture, and so, the state is a major Buddhist pilgrimage site, housing a surfeit of monasteries. Key Monastery (Spiti), Dhankar Monastery (Spiti), Tsuglagkhang Monastery (Dharamshala), Palpung Sherabling Monastery (Palampur), Tabo Monastery (Spiti) are some of the most commonly visited monasteries among many others in Himachal Pradesh.
