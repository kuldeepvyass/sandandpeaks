Singapore Tourism

Settled on the southernmost location of the Malay Peninsula, the diamond-shaped sovereign island city is one of the busiest ports in the world. Its location puts the island right above the equator which has a direct impact on Singapore’s year-round climatic conditions.

The dominating high temperatures and the persistent monsoon season result in one expecting rainfall every day on one part of the island or the other. These fertile monsoon dominating conditions are also the cause of Singapore’s colorful and exotic populations of flora and fauna. While birds like the Indian mynah soar above its topography, several varieties of reptiles slither across the coast.

A Palembang prince’s puzzlement may have led to the country’s christening as Singapura, which means “lion city” in Sanskrit but Singapore didn’t have any lions at the time. However, now, one can find various counterparts of the cat family here.

The island country is formed by the coming together of a total of 63 smaller islands. Sentosa Island and Pulau Ubin are some of the most commonly known names.

The “lion city” is the least corrupt city in Asia. A number of strict laws and regulations prevailing here enforce its cleanliness and a sense of order.

There are a lot of brain-wracking facts about this place. If you’re planning to fly to Singapore for your next vacation, then it’s time to jot some crucial points down for your own benefit.

Keep reading to find out all the dos and donts that need to be kept in check in Singapore.

How to Reach

By Air



Changi Airport is a well-known destination that has often been upvoted as the world’s best airport, owing to access to all kinds of resources and facilities.

Through this airport, Singapore is connected to various international locations from Australia, Asia, North America, Europe, etc. In addition to that, most Indonesian flights usually make a pit stop at Singapore, ultimately leading to more flights heading towards Singapore.

By Train



Via the Tanjong Pagar MRT station in Singapore, travel can be facilitated between Singapore and other locations like Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, and Johor Bahru.

By Road



Asia's mainland and Malaysia are connected to Singapore via two causeways. Transport operators, namely Causeway Link and SBS make the journey possible as their buses run from 5 am to midnight.

By Water



Singapore’s status as an island country has made cruises a popular mode of transport. Ferry services to Riau Islands (Indonesia) and Tanjung Belungkor (Malaysia) operate from Singpore.

Network Connectivity Status

According to a survey conducted by Open Signal in 2020, Singapore topped the list of countries in the Asian Pacific region for Video Experience. Three out of four prominent network providers i.e. M1, StarHub, and Singtel have contributed to an excellent video streaming experience among the users with almost no buffering. The fourth one, TPG, was also responsible for a good experience, but a notch lower than the other three.

However, in terms of 4G network availability, TPG took the crown as the users found their 4G signal to be in reach 97% of the time while they were connected. The other didn’t fall far behind and stayed connected more than 90% of the time. 4G coverage on the other hand showed varied results as this time Singlet and StarHub turned out to be the champions, but TPG and M1 followed closely.

All in all, Singapore’s connectivity is nearly impeccable. If you want to keep using Indian networks, you have to opt for international roaming packages. Otherwise, Jio, Airtel, etc won’t work there.

Top 5 Places to Eat

Odette



Run by Chef Julien Royer, Odette is a three Michelin star restaurant. The restaurant was named after Royer’s grandmother and is now situated alongside the artistry of the National Gallery Singapore. The Chef has earned a name for the restaurant by experimenting and fusing French cuisine with Asian cuisine, ultimately rewarding the place with several awards and also resulting in them topping Asia’a 50 Best Restaurants list in 2020.

Les Amis



Another three Michelin starred restaurant in Singapore that is a huge name is Les Amis under the command of Chef Sebastien Lepinoy. With its extensive collection of wine and a sophisticated ambiance and impressive service, the Les Amis menu lays out sumptuous meals that pitch a strong case for people who are into comfortable and luxurious dine-in.

JAAN



This restaurant is being helmed by Chef Kirk Westaway and his “Tomato Collection” aggrandizes the place’s reputation. Again, if you prefer fine-dining eateries, this should be your go-to choice. The gourmet restaurant is credited with two Michelin stars and has gone through major levels of transformation from excelling at French cuisine to modern European and now British additions have also been made under Westaway’s purview.

Imperial Treasure Super Peking Duck



Peking Duck is an emblematic dish of Beijing. Its first-rate rendition being served at the Imperial Treasure has taken the island by storm.

The restaurant has its own refined elegance with dark wood round tables being part of the seating plan. The exquisiteness of the food servings is what grants the restaurant its reputation and due.

Rang Mahal



Established in 1971, Rang Mahal is one of the top-notch Indian restaurants in Singapore that you can visit to devour homely delights when you’re missing the Indian subcontinent flavor.

Like a typical Indian food platter, your meal is never complete until it is topped off with a decadent dessert. The fine-dining restaurant’s Gulab Jamun Flambé or The Lychee Kulfi are some of the savory spectacles that you can’t miss out on.
An Important Note: Despite the ample availability of Michelin-starred restaurants in Singapore, you must not give up on relishing the local food of Singapore. In order to have a taste of the authentic local experience, you need to visit hawker centres like Lau Pa Sat.
The place offers a huge variety of food options displayed on satay stalls, something that is amiss in the above-mentioned luxury restaurant chains.

Top 5 Dishes You Must Taste

Laksa



This hot rice noodle-based dish is a fusion of Malay and Chinese cuisines. A public favorite, commonly eaten for breakfast and its preparation requires ingredients like coconut milk, chili paste, and fish stock. Seafood like prawns, fishcakes, etc. is the side dish accompanying Laksa.

Satay



Influenced by Indonesian and Indian food standards, satay is an extremely common choice of street food found at hawker food stalls in Singapore. Skewered and marinated chicken, mutton, etc are grilled and served with a chutney or sauce, and sometimes even with rice cakes.

Char Kway Teow



Another favorite noodle dish in Singapore is Char Kway Teow. However, herein, the noodles are white and broad and cooked with soya sauce, Chinese sausage, bean sprouts, fish cake. The name of the dish can be split for its meaning - stir-fried (char) and flat rice noodles (kway teow)

Hokkien Mee



This noodle delicacy originated in the Chinese province Fujian but is now quite popular in Singapore and Malaysia as well. The noodles are cooked with prawn and pork stock. Both rice vermicelli and yellow egg noodles are used to complete the delicacy, adding extra taste to the prominent delicacy famously prepared by the hawker food stalls all over Singapore.

Orh Luak



Having other names o-a-tsian or o-chien, Orh Luak is basically an oyster omelet. Its preparation varies as per the personal preference of the eaters who choose to eat it either as a soft and chewy omelet or crispy. Chilli sauce and lime accentuate the flavor of the recipe.

Local Culture and People

Singapore’s society is a result of the coming together of various influences and cultures, namely South Asian, Southeast Asian, Malay, and Eurasian. Some British aspects can also be observed in people’s mannerisms. As a result, the citizens follow an umpteen number of religions and speak numerous languages.

Though the national language is Malay, other commonly spoken languages are English, Chinese (Mandarin), Tamil.

The dominant religion followed here is Chinese Buddhism. Hinduism and Christianity’s followers are also on the rise.

The country is famous for its cleanliness and in order to maintain that title, several laws have been put into action that prevent people from littering around. One can’t simply carry chewing gums without a medical prescription. No Smoking zones have been allotted. Spitting and urinating in public is another general offense.

Common etiquettes for behavioral conduct in sync with the Singaporean customs that one should keep in mind before engaging in any interaction are- you must be mindful of treating all individuals equally. Additionally, gender discrimination can land you up with quite a few legal charges in Singapore. People here accord one’s head with sacred qualities so touching someone’s head can be seen as an offensive act.
Moreover, you’re bound to eat at restaurants in public with chopsticks. Don’t stick them up vertically in your rice bowl as the image is suggestive of Buddhist funeral rites.

Best Time to Visit

The tropical climate of this island country makes way for a steady summer throughout the entire year. Therefore, it’s totally up to you when you want to plan your trip to Singapore. In such a case scenario, one should keep a tab on the fancy happenings of the place around the year.

Foodies would be delighted by Singapore’s sight during March and April as both months welcome celebratory events like the World Gourmet Summit. Furthermore, the Singapore International Jazz Festival is held in April, also the month most ideal for people looking for lodgings on a tight budget.

August to October is another timeframe that celebrates special days like Singapore National Day, the Beerfest Asia, and the most notable event of all - the Grand Prix.

List of Essentials You Need For the Trip

Packing your essentials can be a bit tricky for Singapore. However, the key is to not stuff your suitcase too much. Whatever you leave behind can be bought in Singapore too without any hassle as the prices are often pocket-friendly.

Although you still need to carry some essentials with you including umbrellas or other rain protection gear like raincoats. Due to its tropical climate and location on the globe, the place receives rain at unexpected times so one needs to be prepared beforehand. The weather tends to heat up quite a bit. Therefore, sunscreens are another important must-have item.

Your passport should have a 6-month validity prior to your Singapore travels. In addition to that, make sure to bring along some copies of the same and save scans of your important documents like passport, flight tickets, ID proofs, bookings, itinerary, etc on your phone.

As per the humid weather that persists throughout the year in Singapore, you should have summer clothing with you. Packing your swimsuits is also necessary, especially if you’re planning to hit the beach or visit water parks.

Basic toiletries and prescribed medicines are always a must.
Finally, visiting a glamorous place like Singapore demands you to capture every bit of it.
Cameras, power banks, and chargers are something we simply can’t do without in this day and age.

Top Locations to Explore in the Vicinity

Sentosa Island



This should’ve been named the fun capital of Singapore considering all the adventurous possibilities that become a reality here.

You can hit the beaches, or dive into your adrenaline rush needs by enjoying the rides at Universal Studios or other waterparks. Jetpacking, Scuba diving, MegaZip, Bungee jumping, you name it, and all these thrill rides are waiting for you around the corner.

Marina Bay



Marina Bay’s platter is full of famous landmarks that reflect Singapore’s name. The Merlion, Marina Bay Sands, Gardens by the Bay, Michelin starred restaurants, affluent shopping complexes deck this place and make up for a great time off on a vacation.

Head over to SkyPark if you want to clear up your headspace while gazing at the top view of the entire city.

Mandai



The Singaporean wildlife residence holds some majestic creatures that will take your breath away at first glance. The Singapore Zoo houses a variety of species including Hamadryas baboons, a huge family of orangutans, and the magnificent white tiger. Other than that, you can join the River safari and bear witness to the world’s biggest freshwater aquarium.

The Night Safari is yet another option that will bless you with visions of nocturnal animals.

Bukit Timah



Also known as Bt Timah, stationed in the westernmost half of Central Singapore is a residential district. The place is originally known for its dreamy and luxury accommodations. In layman’s terms, it’s the one place where everyone wants to live.

What makes it an even more enriching quarter to settle down in is its surrounding natural greenery. Bukit Timah Nature Reserve is the last standing rainforest of Singapore.

Clarke Quay



If flashy and hip events are your calling, then you will have a blast in Clarke Quay. Explore galleries and exhibitions at the Asian Civilisations Museum or sip in the nightlife while drifting through the currents of the Singapore River.

The G-Max Reverse Bungee is one of the most sought-after activities of adrenaline junkies. There are quite a few clubs here like Canvas, Attica, Crazy Elephant, and more, where you can dance the night away.

Bugis-Bras Basah



Once here, you’ll be transported to a separate micro-artistic universe of its own. The streets of Haji Lane are lined up with cafes and boutiques while the walls are covered with murals. You will feel as if it’s all part of a movie shoot, where you elegantly stride across this avant-garde setting.

Aliwal Arts Centre, Malay Heritage Centre, the National Design Centre are some of the modern establishments here displaying art and culture at its best.

Little India



Singapore’s own Desi enclave is home to a huge population of Indians or South Asians. The district is lively as ever but it lights up a bit extra, especially during Indian festivities like Diwali celebrations. The restaurants and shops set up in the area cater to the Desi demographic.

Situated across Chinatown, this community is a microcosmic representation of the Indian subcontinent and its cultural diversity. The 24 hours running Mustafa Centre even has saree shops.

MacRitchie Reservoir



Completed in 1868, MacRitchie is the oldest reservoir in Singapore. It offers various activities as a means of recreation. Tourists will have an amazing time away from their hectic schedules as they can go hiking, kayaking, freshwater fishing, and just be enchanted by the overall scenic and calming surroundings of the place.

Pulau Ubin



This place is a lush green paradise for flora-fauna lovers. Situated to the northeast of Singapore’s mainland, this island has preserved the older village-like ambiance with magnificent birds like bulbul and Kingfisher also residing here.

You can engage in soul-soothing activities like bird-watching, cycling, and rejuvenating amidst the wetlands or be at peace in the untouched environs of Ubin’s beaches.

Chinatown



Being the primary residence area for Chinese immigrants, Chinatown is famous is for the representation of various Asian cultures, their cuisines, etc.
Thian Hock Keng Temple, Singapore’s first temple was also established here. Other Buddhist temples, shopping complexes, and budget-friendly food eateries pull tourists to this location.

Top Attractions in the Vicinity

Marina Bay Sands



Starting off with a rather upscale resort located right across Marina Bay, this hotel harbors the world’s largest rooftop infinity pool that looks over the breathtaking views of Singapore. Deluxe rooms of this hotel offer an otherworldly experience while world-class dining serves you.

Some other activities held within include the ArtScience Museum, Sampan Rides, SkyPark Observation Deck, and more, that’ll imbue your reality with cinematic spectacles.

Fort Canning Park



Locals have associated superstitions with this historical landmark of Singapore, leading them to call it the Forbidden Hill (hill Bukit Larangan). At a height of 48 m, this small hill acts as the resting place of the ancient kings that ruled over Singapore.

Sultan Iskandar Shah, the last king of Singapura is enshrined at an establishment called Keramat Iskandar Shah. However, now in the modern age, the place has turned into a likely venue for art festivals, carnivals, and other performances due to the lush green palatial lawns covering the area.

Orchard Road



A paradise for shoppers, Orchard Road extends to a route of 2.5 km. It’s not only one of the oldest but also the most sought-after high-end shopping destinations in Singapore. Several restaurants, cafes, and stores make up this complex.

This boulevard offers numerous options for one’s entertainment. Other than shopping, youngsters can be seen skateboarding at the Somerset Skate Park. You can take a glance at the relics of the Peranakan culture; try your hand at martial arts like Jiu-Jitsu and others at Evolve MMA, or visit art galleries.

Merlion Park



The prime visual synonymous with Singapore’s image is the 8.6 m tall statue of a fantastical half-lion, half-fish creature. This glorious icon of the city can be witnessed at Merlion Park, near Marina Bay.

Gardens by the Bay



Covering 250 acres of land, Gardens by the Bay is a nature park, located right next to the Marina reservoir. The famous landmark displays horticulture and its garden arrangement with a new pair of eyes. Tourists can visit the world’s largest glass greenhouse i.e Flower Dome, walk underneath the towering grand trees, learn about the place’s heritage and exotic varieties of plants, etc.

Universal Studios



Southeast Asian countries don’t have their fair share of movie-themed parks. Not to worry because Universal Studios Singapore is the first of its kind. Jaw-dropping rides, live entertainment shows, and other alluring attractions pull tourists to its various themed zones where they can live their fantasy of being a part of their favorite movie-verse.

Jewel Changi Airport



Often taking the spot as the world’s best airport, Singapore’s Changi airport has a retail complex - Jewel- that has become one of the major attractions of the city. The place is dotted with indoor gardens and its beauty is centered around the Rain Vortex, i.e the world’s tallest indoor waterfall.

National Gallery Singapore



The largest collection of Southeast Asian modern art finds its home at the National Gallery Singapore. Spanning up to 64,000 sq m, the large venue display Southeast Asian art pieces which boast of its expansive culture, heritage, and glory.

Fort Siloso



Preserving the symbols commemorating the lived action of the Battle of Singapore, Fort Siloso is the only standing coastal fort in Singapore that has now been converted into a national monument owing to its military past.

Must-Do Activities You Can Enjoy in the Vicinity

Reverse Bungy



At Clarke Quay, adventure enthusiasts can find their way to one of the most striking activities in Singapore. The joiners of this thrill ride are elevated to a height of almost 60 m and then drastically pulled down at a speed of 200 km/hr.

Megazip



The Mega Adventure Park located on Sentosa Island harbors one of the main exciting attractions of Singapore i.e. the Megazip flying fox. This 450 m long zip line allows visitors to fly at a speed of 60 km/hr, encompassing the picturesque beauty of the Island.

Paragliding



Competitions related to paragliding are a regular feature in Singapore and so it’s not merely a recreational activity here. Exclusive training programs for the adventure sport are also open for interested people.

Jetpack



With a backpack fastened onto the back of the adventurer, jetpacks allow people to be airborne at a height of around 30m across waters. You can give it a whirl at Sentosa Island where safety measures are the utmost priority and the personnel even goes into detailed training for the same. Only people older than 16 yrs are allowed to make an attempt.

Scuba Diving



A lot of water sports find their ideal hub in Singapore. Scuba diving happens to be another one that makes the place worth visiting. SEA Aquarium on Sentosa Island is the place you should be headed to if you want to feel the rush of this amazing sport.

Indoor Skydiving



Sentosa Island is home to quite a few riveting activities and adventures. Adding another to the list, iFly Singapore should be your destination to find the world’s largest indoor skydiving win tunnel. This intriguing yet exhilarating setup is a safer alternative for all those who are scared of real skydiving.

Witnessing the Spectacle of Wings of Time Show



Sentosa Island has a great many gems to offer and Wings of Time show is one of those displays that have become an extravaganza. It’s an outdoor night show that showcases waterworks and fireworks alike, adding to the holiday mood of tourists. This modern spectacle will be a colorful finale to your Singaporean sojourn.
