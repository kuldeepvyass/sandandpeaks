Maldives Tourism

In the midst of the Indian ocean, the Maldivian archipelago is surrounded by azure waters that make for a heavenly vision unlike another.

It’s a place where eye-catching polychromatic coral reefs are yet preserved as treasure troves; human intervention is amiss and manmade creations don’t seek to annihilate the perennial flow of nature’s graceful existence.

Calling it a paradise may seem cliche, but that’s exactly what it truly is.

The island has its own tales of historical significance that have often been left unsung. Other than the obvious attraction of the exciting sandy beaches of Maldives, the country is a gem of a source to learn many things.

Before booking tickets for your trip to the Maldives, there is a lot of information that you need to be aware of to avoid any sort of mishaps befalling you there. The country is fairly conservative due to the prevalence of Islamic customs. Moreover, a whole list of items is banned there as well.

We’ve curated this in-depth breakdown of all that you need to know before landing on the Maldivian soil. Read on to decode the major tips you need to follow for your trip to the Maldives to be a grand success.

How to Reach

By Air



Travelling by air is the most ideal option for reaching the island nation. Male International Airport (also known as Velana International Airport) is the major airport in the Maldives that facilitates covering the distance between other places on the globe, with the South-East Asian countries like Sri Lanka, India, China being the closest neighbors.

Male International Airport is in proximity to Male as it's based in Hulhule Island (North Male Atoll).

The various functioning airlines connecting the Maldives to other countries are Qatar Airways, Air India, Emirates, Air France, Turkish Airlines, and more. In addition to that, “Maldivian”, i.e. Maldives’ national airline also makes international as well as domestic travels possible.

By Sea



Another luxurious transportation that will uplift your vacation mood is a Cruise.

Several cruises depart from Mumbai and Kochi in India for the Maldives. Depending on budget options, you can choose from the available options to embark on your journey to the blue paradise.

A few of these cruise options are Costa Cruise Lines (Costa NeoClassica), a lavish ride that keeps your budget on the table. Its route takes the passengers from Mumbai to New Mangalore to Kochi and then finally Male.
Another pocket-friendly cruise is Louis Cruise (MV Aquamarine) which commences its voyage from Kochi to the Maldives.

Some others on the list include Jalesh Cruise and Noble Caledonia (MS Island Sky ZE).

Network Connectivity Status

Maldives has two major network providers - Dhiraagu and Ooredoo. Both providers offer prepaid and postpaid plans and their connectivity is more or less the same regardless of your location on the island. However, the postpaid plan can only be availed by Maldivian locals.

There are some minor differences between the two providers. Firstly, Dhiraagu has proved to serve wider coverage while Ooredoo has mostly been opted by locals residing on islands best for living purposes.

Top 5 Places to Eat

Meraki Coffee Roasters



The addictive coffee aroma here will have a bewitching effect on you that you’d never want to leave. The interior lighting and brick walls set up a mood taken straight out of a cafe setting from a movie. Even the basic cafe orders like Cappucino and Latte are must-haves here due to the heavenly touch of the cafe. Other options you should look out for are Cascara tea with honey, cookies, cold brew coffee.

Jazz Cafe



The name says it all. At Jazz Cafe, the music plays along as one awaits their serving of authentic Maldivian preparations. The posh ambiance uplifts the sensory experience and offers a soothing break from one’s daily schedule.

The must-try list includes Roshi, Yellow Chicken Curry, Roast Chicken with barbecue sauce, and a lot more. Other international cuisines are also available, however, the Maldivian delicacies are the real stars.

Reethi Restaurant



Luxury at its finest indeed! The dining setup is a visual treat with its three divisions - Fire, Aqua, and Earth, facing the oceanic views. The restaurant serves a fusion of several delectable cuisines and days are allotted for specifically serving one of them.

Italian Mondays and Indian Fridays have an irresistible hold over the visitors. Their exquisite and vast wine collection is yet another attraction for wine enthusiasts.

Conrad Maldives Rangali Island



Three words - Underwater private dining. What else could you ask for the perfect romantic evening with your beloved partner?

With its luxurious resort, Conrad Maldives Rangali Island has a lot to offer like private pools, spas, water sports, etc. It’s an otherworldly experience accompanied with the best of their delicacies recommended by past diners - the Sunset Grill, lobster, and champagne barbecue.

Just Veg



Located in Atmosphere Kanifushi Resort (Lhaviyani Atoll), Just Veg is the best and the first purely vegetarian eatery in the Maldives. The restaurant experiments with multiple cuisines from the Indian Ocean and Mediterranean localities and serves a surprising appetizing blend that will leave you awestruck.

Top 5 Dishes You Must Taste

Gulha



Gulha is a typical snack eaten in the Maldives in which ball-shaped wheat or rice flour dough is stuffed with tuna fish, chilies, coconut, and onions. This fried delicacy has earned quite a name among the locals who often add curry leaves, lime juice, and turmeric to make the filling even more enriching. People tend to enjoy it with warm beverages like coffee or tea.

Saagu Bondibai



Maldives’s location as an island gives rise to a significant amount of non-vegetarian food options. However, that doesn’t mean there aren’t any prominent vegetarian options. Saagu Bondibai is one such dish made of boiled saagu seeds and is accompanied by coconut milk in its preparation. Once the condensed milk and rose and cardamom are also added, the dessert leaves you mesmerized.

Aluvi Boakibaa



Here’s yet another dessert from the Maldives that you just can’t resist. It’s basically a cassava cake made of coconut. Both locals and tourists love it equally, owing to the extent of its popularity it is readily available at restaurants. It’s also a festive sweet and is particularly prepared during the celebrations of Ramadan.

Mas Huni



Usually taken as a popular breakfast meal, Mas Huni is a Tuna based dish in which its shredded pieces are put together with coconut, onion, and chilies. It’s commonly eaten with a flatbread called Roshi.

Fish curry



This one undoubtedly tops the Maldivian menus. Freshwater fish is abundantly visible in the waters of Maldives and so Fish Curry has become a part of the staple diet here. The main ingredients required to successfully prepare this concoction are fish, starch, and coconut. It’s generally paired up with flatbread aka Roshi, rice, or Maldivian poppadoms.

Local Culture and People

Naturally the lives of the people living on the island nation Maldives are governed by the water bodies surrounding it. Most of their traditional cuisine is derived from the sea just as most of their livelihoods.

The dominant cultural values prevailing here are an assimilation of various influences i.e. Indian, Arab, African, Indonesian, Malaysian, Persian, and Sri Lankan. The official religion following is Islamic and its laws form the structural integrity of the nation’s culture.

Dhivehi is the official language and just like Arab languages, its script is also written from right to left.

Due to its major Islam following demographic, the island celebrates Eid with extra fervor.

Moreover, the multitude of influential neighboring countries has resulted in a rich culture of the musical performing arts. Bodu Beru is one of the most famous Maldivian performances in which a group of approximately 20 men participates.

Interacting with the locals is a holistic experience in itself as their warm welcoming embrace adds to the positive attitude towards your stay in the Maldives.

Best Time to Visit

Considering the tropical conditions of the Maldives, it isn’t ideal to engage in water sports during the monsoon waves. Therefore the best time to travel to the island is from January to the first half of April, which makes up for the driest time frame during the whole year. Days tend to remain hot and sunny and skies are mostly clear during this time period and make for the best conditions for you to delve into outdoor activities.

Nevertheless, one should be wary of the occasional thunderstorms during this period as well, especially if your accommodations are situated on the southern islands.

List of Essentials You Need For the Trip

While it’s a good idea to list down your essentials for the trip it’s even more important to make a note of all the things you shouldn’t be carrying with you to the Maldives.

The country has laid out a list of prohibited items that can’t be brought into the Maldives. These include narcotics, idols for worship, alcohols, dogs, gun powder, spear guns, chemicals. The Maldivian society is an avid practitioner of Islamic law, therefore, you must remember to dress up conservatively. You can change into the attires of your choice once you’ve reached the resort.

The tropical climatic conditions of the place also demand one to cover up with scarves to protect oneself from the harsh heat. Sunscreen is another necessity you should be mindful of.

You’ll be heading out various underwater activities, so it’s better to bring along a waterproof camera to capture all those delightful memories.

Remember to carry your basic first aid kit and medicines at all costs.

Your important documents like Identity proofs, passports, airplane tickets, etc need to be with you at all times possible. Traveling to an international destination can be a tricky endeavor if you forget to add these crucial items to your list.

Top Locations to Explore in the Vicinity

Baros Island



Baros is on the affluent end of island chains in the Maldives but that’s also what makes it one of the most peaceful options to visit. Its cyan-blue waters along with several other amenities made available here like luxurious spas, resorts, villas along with enthralling watersports like snorkeling and scuba diving make it an ideal location that helps push away all your doubts and worries.

Male City



The renowned capital of the island nation is a cultural and historical hub housing several pilgrimage centers and other monuments of paramount importance. Its island is divided into the North Male Atoll and South Male Atoll and used to be known as ‘Mahal’ since Maldives’ royal families resided here.

The Maldivian capital is the most sought-after tourist destination of the country with a multitude of attractions like the Artificial Beach, Male Fish Market, Sultan Park, National Museum, and more captivating the tourists’ interest.

Majeedhee Magu



Maldives’ own shopaholic heaven located in the capital Male is a great place to shop for souvenirs. You name it and it's all there - local handicrafts, fashion attires, the traditional Maldivian ‘libaas’, perfumes, varieties of fish snacks, and a lot more. To top it all off, a few of the best cafes in the Maldives are also situated here. You can grab a quick bite at Seagull Cafe or Juways Cafe, after all, food does make it all better.

Addu Atoll



The southernmost atoll of the Maldives, the heart-shaped Addu Atoll accomodating various species of fish and mammals is also known as the Seenu Atoll. It’s located at a distance of 335 miles from Male City. This barely inhabited island has its own historical significance hinged in the past of World War 2 when it was used as a secret base.

COMO Cocoa Island



Topping all of the Maldivian tourist destinations is COMO Cocoa. This island is decked with around 33 water villas that offer the best amenities and services, thereby granting your wish of pampering yourself on your most awaited vacation. Therapeutic spa treatments and water activities make it a must

Hanifaru Bay



Baa Atoll’s Hanifaru Bay is a UNESCO biosphere reserve and therefore a Marine Protected Area. Manta rays can be found here in abundance in addition to the sightings of whale sharks. The bay is especially famous for activities like night snorkeling and scuba diving in the company of these sublime creatures.

Fulhadhoo Island



Yet another prized gem of the Baa Atoll, Fulhadhoo Island is a peaceful isolated island away from other congested tourist attractions. The island’s starstudded beauty is marked by its numerous lagoons and natural reefs, untouched by human interference.

Mihiri Island



Located in the Alifi Dhaal Atol, Mihiri Island is like the digital detox center of the Maldives. The island particularly attracts honeymooners as its environment encourages people to put aside their virtual reality and prioritize real-life interactions. It’s a sure-shot romantic getaway as partners can enjoy relaxing spa treatments together and also dive into other water sport options available here.

The Glowing Beach



In Mudhdhoo island (or Vaadhoo island), the natural phenomenon of Bioluminescence has birthed a luminescent body of water. This glow-in-the-dark effect is caused by the bioluminescent phytoplankton inhabiting the beach. A mesmerizing fantasy-like vision will leave you enchanted. You must visit this beach once or it’ll just be a greatly missed opportunity.

HP Reef



A designated diving site in the Maldives, HP Reef is located at the North Male Atoll. It’s often referred to as the Rainbow Reef or the Girifushi Thila due to the presence of the colorful varieties of corals settled at the seabed. HP Reef’s resplendent beauty is one of a kind and so has been declared as a Protected Marine Area as well.

Top Attractions in the Vicinity

Whale Submarine



Are you afraid to go scuba diving or snorkeling, but still want to witness the underwater mysteries of the Maldives? If yes, then this is the best underwater activity for you. A 45-minute long tour in the whale submarine will allow you to catch a glimpse of the majestic underwater life of shark rays, turtles, Yellow Boxfish, Common Lionfish.

Grand Friday Mosque



The golden-domed attraction in Male was built in 1984 and is now a part of the Islamic Centre. Maldives’ biggest mosque was named after Sultan Muhammad Thakurufaanu-Al-Auzam. The architectural design of the establishment is adorned with Arabic calligraphy and is thus not merely a pilgrimage center but also a place loved by architecture enthusiasts.

National Museum



Sultan Park’s National Museum used to be the Royal Palace of Maldives in the 17th-century. The museum marks the historical trajectory of the island right from the Buddhist predominance to Muslim monarchs taking over. A huge collection of antique artifacts is displayed here in addition to Maldives’ first telephone and gramophone. The collection exhibited in the museum serves as a reminder of the Maldives’ conversion to Islam.

Tsunami Monument



The Tsunami Monument was erected in Thin Ruh Park (southeast Male) to commemorate the ravages brought forth by the 2004 tsunami. The symbolic architecture of the monument has twenty steel spheres representing the twenty Maldivian atolls. The establishment even displays the names of those who lost their lives on that fateful day.

The Kongannu Cemetery



Located in Addu City is Maldives’ oldest cemetery, dating back to 900 years ago. It entombs the first Muslims residing in the Addu Atoll. The cemetery is also famous for having the largest standing tombstone in the Maldives.

Male Friday Mosque



Also known as the Old Friday Mosque or Hukuru Miskiiy, Male Friday opened in 1658 and is one of the oldest mosques in the Maldives. Arabic script is engraved on the walls of the structure and its architecture attracts tourists from around the world, also the reason for the place being declared as a UNESCO World Heritage SIte in 2008.

National Art Gallery



In 1999, the Maldivian government made way for the highly treasured National Art Gallery housing local as well as international works of art. The artifacts held by the Gallery showcase an amalgamation of cultural representation through which artists can convey their artistic expressions to the audience.

The Muraka - Conrad



Part of the Conrad Maldives Resort on Rangali Island, the Muraka is an underwater villa.
Staying here is a surreal experience with the cyanic waters of the Indian Ocean surrounding you on all sides. Visitors can make the most of this location by reveling in activities like snorkeling, jet skiing, and diving. The fancy accommodation has loads of high-rated services to offer like private chefs, amazing spa treatments, its own infinity pool to name a few.

China-Maldives Friendship Bridge



Sinamale Bridge or China Maldives Friendship Bridge was built as a mark of the standing friendship ties between the two nations. The bridge is extremely wide with two 4-wheeler lanes and four 2-wheeler lanes. The Maldivian government even sanctioned a few weddings to take place here in the past.

Victory Monument



This white-colored monument erected in Male commemorates the Maldivian victory over the militant uprising of Tamil Tigers. It has been established far away from the bustling city landscape and so people often come here in pursuit of a peaceful getaway from their daily schedules.

Must-Do Activities You Can Enjoy in the Vicinity

Surfing



The waters of Maldives may be worth saving as your lock screen but their visual appeal can never be justified by a camera capturing it.

There are three surfing zones - Northern, Central, and Southern Atolls. Each of them offers the ideal surfing spots depending on the seasonal changes. While the Southern Atolls are best for surfing during the stretch between March and April, it’s suitable for you to head over to the Northern or Central Atoll from May to October.

Snorkeling



Water sports are the first fun activity people associate with the image of Maldives. Snorkeling is one of the most popular underwater adventures tourists choose to embark on to uncover the hidden mysteries of the countless reefs on this island country.
A few of the best places for this activity are - Kuda Kalhi (Banana Reef), Lankanfinolhu Island (Manta Point), Male Atoll (HP Reef), etc.

Dolphin Watching



Watching the extremely intelligent mammals playing around in crystal clear waters is quite a delight. You need to be mindful of the time for this recreational activity. Mornings and afternoons are the ideal time for you to witness these magnificent creatures frolicking around.

And most importantly, remember to keep your distance. Making happy sounds by clapping your hands or so will attract them to you. Don’t try to feed them by throwing any food at them, it’s likely to drive them away.

Relaxing Spa Treatment



Exotic spa treatments aren’t just relegated to the regime of relaxation anymore, they extend fitness and wellness impacts as well. The Maldives has gained its stature as one of the most sought-after spa destinations as it was even voted by the World Spa Awards for the same in 2017. Spas also proved to have desirable effects on one’s skincare and so, Duniye Spa held at Hurawalhi Maldives Resort even received acclaim at the World Luxury Spa Awards in 2016.

The comforting combination of massages, yoga sessions, and skincare routines helps one destress and detoxify.

Parasailing



The paradisiacal views of Maldives’ natural beauty when glanced at from an elevated point of view allow you to take it all in at one go. Flying in a parachute while a speed boat pulls you is an extraordinary and refreshing activity that enlivens your spirits upon being surrounded by the clear sparkling waters of the ocean.
