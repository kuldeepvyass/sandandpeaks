Spiti Valley

Spiti Valley refers to “midde land”, which falls in place since it literally lies between Tibet and India. This valley located within Himachal Pradesh is one of the remotest and least populated areas in India. Owing to this fact, the place beholds the natural marvels and beauty of the cold barren desert, marred by lush green patches in certain regions. These scenic and charming visions have the capability of leaving individuals wonderstruck.

Despite its overall muddy and arid landscape, Spiti is a safe haven whose residents follow traditions and cultural festivities as per the Ladakhi and Tibetan influence. And, it’s due to this religious and cultural heritage that Spiti Valley acts as a gateway for one’s spiritual awakening, away from the polluted and heavily congested surroundings of metropolitan cities.

This place is also the last standing ground that provides a secure home to the Buchen Lamas of the Nyingmapa sect of Buddhism.

Herman Melville said, “It is not down in any map; true places never are”. Similarly, you will encounter many such undiscovered locations in Spiti that no one’s ever heard of before. It’s the land of the unknown, and as you embark on the journey to explore such hidden mysteries, you may end up finding yourself too.

There’s a lot in store waiting for you at Spiti Valley. In order to come up with a meticulous itinerary for your trip, we’ve detailed everything you need to know before packing your bags.

How to Reach

Due to the cold desert’s remote location, arriving at the final destination can be quite challenging. And regardless of which mode of transport you opt for, traveling via road is inevitable in the end. A detailed route for traveling via train, flight, or road to Spiti valley is listed out below.

By Train

There are around three ways to reach Spiti Valley when traveling via train. The nearest metro station to Spiti Valley is Joginder Nagar Railway Station, located at a distance of 360 km. If taking Manali as the point of reference then the station is 160 km away from there.
Another feasible route is via Shimla since Jogindernagar isn’t as well connected as other railway stations. The Shimla Railway Station is another alternative (the distance between Shimla and Spiti Valley is about 416 km). To reach here you’ll have to first board the train destined to Kalka from either Chandigarh or Delhi. Then board another train from Kalka to Shimla, and from Shimla get on board a bus or cab to finally reach Spiti Valley.
Nevertheless, the best way out of all these routes is from Chandigarh since it’s the least complicated one. First, you’ll have to board a train to Chandigarh and then by either a bus or taxi reach Spiti Valley.

By Flight

Air journey to Spiti Valley is again a bit complex and arduous as the nearest airport to Spiti Valley is 260 km away in Kullu. The Kullu airport is also merely a domestic airport so direct flights may not always be possible. Upon reaching Kullu, one needs to travel for about 8 hours to reach Spiti Valley.

Otherwise, Chandigarh has the nearest international airport, which is again quite far from Spiti Valley.

By Road

No matter which means of transport you choose, you will ultimately have to travel via road to reach your final destination.

Traveling from various cities like Delhi, Chandigarh, Manali, Shimla, etc to Spiti Valley is possible almost year-round (however, it’s dangerous due to landslides and slippery roads during Winters). One can either take the Manali route going through Rohtang Pass; the Shimla route or the Srinagar route, passing through Kargil.

Network Connectivity Status

The high-altitude location of Spiti Valley is the reason for the regular disruption in network connectivity here. In 2021, residents of the area even complained regarding this matter to higher authorities.
Lack of internet connectivity has had a disheartening impact during the pandemic, especially on the students as their education is on the line.

Many secluded villages like Lari village have witnessed this case of failing internet connectivity.

Mobile towers of BSNL and Jio have been stationed in Tabo. Therefore, one has to travel there to catch a signal and better connectivity.

Although both pre-paid and post-paid connections function here, out of all the network providers, BSNL or MTNL services offer better coverage. Airtel and Jio’s connectivity is mostly limited from Kinnaur Valley to Spillow.

BSNL and Airtel networks are accessible in Reckong Peo, Sangla, Chitkul, and Kalpa. However, routes like Chandratal to Losar or Gramphu to Batal have no connectivity at all.

Under any circumstance, it’s advisable to intimate your family and friends beforehand and prepare for bad network connectivity.

Top 5 Places to Eat

It’s a commonly noticed feature that most prominent cafes are located in Kaza. Therefore, Kaza should be your go-to choice to satisfy your cravings for delicious bites.

The Himalayan Cafe

Located in the main market of Kaza, you will find this eatery topping everyone’s list of restaurants you must visit in Spiti. It’s a favorite local hangout spot whose menu includes scrumptious multi-cuisine options. At night, the place is decked with bright lanterns and fairy lights, and its ambience is welcoming with sweet notes of music in the background.

Dragon Restaurant

This is another popular food joint in Kaza (adjacent to the bus station) that you must try. The place serves Tibetan as well as Indian cuisines and the quality of the food is finger-licking good.

Yak Cafe

Adding to the list of appetizing restaurant choices at Kaza, Yak Cafe is a renowned name in the neighborhood. The cafe serves Tibetan, Chinese and some other food options and is open for large gatherings. Due to its proximity to Old Kaza, the location of the cafe is easily accessible.

Cafe Kunzum Top

There’s just no way that you can go about your way without trying Kunzum Top’s coffee. The eatery can be found near Tabo Monastery and it is the ideal place for you to dig into Tibetan cuisine. The cafe also has its connected guesthouse - Sonam Homestay, which is a suitable option in terms of budget and other hospitable services.

Sol Cafe

The hot beverages served in this cafe managed by Ecosphere volunteers are a delight unlike any other. Coupling them up with dishes like waffles, pancakes and french toast acts as the perfect combination to soothe one’s heart in Spiti’s cold desert valley. You can also refill your water bottles free of charge. Moreover, you can sit back and relax with your friends during the movie nights hosted here.

Top 5 Dishes You Must Taste
Dhaam

This is basically what we refer to as a full-fledged thali in North India. Initially, it was a meal reserved for the Himachali Brahmins and would be served to them on leaves. Every special occasion or festival is put together by preparing this meal, for which “botis” (chefs who cater to this specific dish) are hired. A nutritious plate of Dhaam is accompanied with the base formed by rice and topped with “masala sabzis” like rajma, kadi, madra, boor ki Badi.

Thukpa

A lot of delicacies specific to Spiti Valley have been influenced by the Tibetan culture and its cuisine. Thukpa is one such item on the food menu of Himachal Pradesh, and it’s widely consumed by the localites. The combination of noodles, some basic cooked vegetables, chicken or beef soup with chutney is filled with enough warmth to charge you up for the Winters.

Madra

One serving of madra is a seasoned amalgamation of chickpeas, some vegetables, and spices, with dahi as its foundation, thereby making it similar to kadi. It’s often eaten along with puris and can be served throughout all three meals of the day.

Siddu

Siddu is the “pahari” local bread made of wheat. Its preparation requires a lot of patience and time. The efforts are worth it in the end as the bread stuffed with numerous fillings is regularly consumed with dal or mutton, and this combination works wonders.

Aktori

This old recipe may now be famous all over Himachal Pradesh but it was born in Lahaul- Spiti. Aktori is quite popular during the festive season. For its preparation, buckwheat leaves are mixed in wheat flour, then the prepared batter is cooked on the pan like a pancake. And so, the Pahari pancake lookalike is often served with honey for better taste.

Local Culture and People

Interaction with the locals of any region is inevitable. Residents of Spiti Valley speak Hindi, Sanskrit and Bodh. The traditions followed by the people are similar to the ones defined in Tibetan and Ladakhi cultures.

As far as religion is concerned, the people follow either the Hindu tenets or are believers of Tibetan Buddhism of the Drukpa order or Gelgpa order. Earlier people also used to the religion called Lung Pe Chhoi, influenced by the Bon religion of Tibet.

Now on to the major festivals celebrated here. Losar festival (or Halda) is celebrated sometime between January and February annually. This date is finalized by the Lamas.

The locals share a deep connection with their religion, which ultimately ties them to their roots and homeland as well. One can correlate this natural bond to a kind of reverence of the land. This is why the moonscape of Spiti has been able to preserve its heavenly charm and beauty and save itself from the destructive clutches of modernization.

Best Time to Visit

Depending on what kind of activities one wants to indulge in and the aura one wants to witness, the itinerary can vary accordingly. While it’s not recommended to travel during the short interval of monsoon season since roads are often blocked due to the damages caused by rainfall, summer and winter both offer a unique window of panoramic views respectively.

The long winter of Spiti Valley is engulfed in a dark and gloomy situation due to the lack of the sun’s warmth and the ubiquitous snow-covered surfaces. Tourists often avoid this time of the year due to its extremely ruthless cold conditions. Nevertheless, it appears to be a wonderland decked with snow in every direction, and some people wish to bear witness to this picturesque vision.

Summertime is when Spiti is most visited by wanderlusts because the snow starts to subside and sightseeing is made possible. The average temperature throughout the day is also pleasant enough for people to take pleasure in the activities they wish to perform. The colorful landscape is visible and various festivities are also organized in the valley at this time. Therefore, the time frame between April - July is the most suitable for coming up with your itinerary for a trip to Spiti Valley.

List of Essentials You Need for Spiti Trip

Before digging into the other half of your suitcase, let’s list down the important documents that you need to carry along with you for this trip. Identity proofs like Aadhar cards, voter ID or Driving License need to be kept handy. Plus, it’s advisable that you bring some photocopies of the same with a few passport-sized photographs.
Without your itinerary you’ll be lost, so make sure to tuck it into your suitcase as well.

For foreign nationals, bringing their passports is extremely essential, and foreigners need a Protected Area Permit to enter the valley.

Inner Line Permit is also required if you’re planning to visit Kaza and Reckong Peo.
Moving on to the clothing. Regardless of which month you travel to this destination, you need to carry fleece jackets and extra thermals. The weather conditions are very unpredictable, even during the Summer season, one can never know when cold winds start brewing.
Even though rain isn’t as common, you should have a raincoat with you.
Insulated boots are a must during Winter, else you may be exposed to frostbite.

Basic first-aid and medicines are something you can’t afford to forget. Antiseptic creams, painkillers, pain relief sprays, Paracetamol, band-aids, medicine for altitude sickness, remedies for blocked nose, etc are but obvious requirements when heading to such a cold location.

Carrying your electronic gadgets like power banks, chargers, cameras, earphones, etc is another common habit, so make a list of everything that will be accompanying you. More importantly, JIO and BSNL are the only effective networks in the mountains, so keep their SIM cards ready for your trip.

Top Locations to Explore in the Vicinity
Kunzum Pass

Resting at an altitude of 15,060 ft above sea level, this pass located in the Kunzum Range of the Himalayas has about 15 acute curving turns which can be quite grueling for drivers. This is one of the most popular motorable passes in India that acts as a gateway to Lahaul and Spiti Valley. Further connected to Manali and Kaza, the pass also has a temple built in the name of Goddess Kunzum Devi, who is believed to be the protector of the travelers passing through here.

Baralacha Pass

Situated in the Zanskar range, this pass runs parallel to the Leh-Manali highway and acts as a channel between Leh and Lahaul. It’s 8 km long and people often use it to facilitate their treks headed to the Chandratal lake. At a height of 16,040 ft, the roads here are well constructed and safe for traveling during the Summers. However, the pass remains closed during the Winter season due to roads getting blocked by heavy snowfall.

Hikkim

People who have a craze for exploring hidden marvels and sites will be overjoyed upon visiting this place. The air of this location is so clean and pure, making it a heavenly refuge from all sorts of metropolitan worries. In addition to that, the world’s highest post office can be found here too.

Nako Village

Tourists visiting this village need the Inner Line permit due to its shared location with the Indo-Tibetan border. It’s one of the lesser-known and isolated villages of the valley. If you wish to spend some time in a peaceful and soothing environment while getting acquainted with the authentic Tibetan and Pahari culture, this is the place to be. Sheltering a huge variety of plants, the Lipa Asrang Sanctuary is located in proximity to the village.

Komik

Being the highest motorable village in the world, this hamlet boasts of mindboggling views. It’s an ideal location for a getaway since being here allows you to cut off ties with your hectic metropolitan schedules. The locals are warm-hearted and will help you look for fossils. You must try the tea served at various stops here.

Tabo

The town of Tabo sits on the banks of the Spiti River at a height of 10,760 ft. Sharing the borders of both Tibet and India, Tabo is renowned for its 1000-year-old monastery which is deemed to be a highly revered base of religion, even by Dalai Lama. With several other ancient temples and caves being situated here, Tabo is one of the most culturally enriching towns in the valley.

Losar

Situated right next to the Indo-Chinese border, the Losar village is at an altitude of 4000 m. The calmingly quiet surroundings of this place will have a healing impact on your psyche and teach you to appreciate living in the moment.

Kibber

The village Kibber located in the higher altitudes of Spiti (at an elevation of 4270 m) is prominently known for housing the Kibber Monastery and Kibber Wildlife Sanctuary. It’s a relatively secluded hamlet with barely 80 houses in the area. Tourists often travel here for experiencing adventure activities like trekking and camping.

Sarchu

Tented accommodations are quite a common feat of Sarchu. The place acts as a mid halting point when traveling from Manali to Leh or vice versa. The original name of Sarchu was Sir Bhum Chun. The place mostly serves the purpose of sightseeing, as one can witness the glory of the barren mountains closely here. Other than that, it’s a good camping site as well.

Udaipur

Located on the banks of Chandrabhaga River, Udaipur is famous for having quite a few pilgrimage sites like Marikula Mata Temple. In the olden times, the place was known as Markul due to the establishment of the temple. The lush green environs of the place also make it a suitable ground for camping. Treks destined to Zanskar, Kibber and Kangla begin from here as well.

Top Attractions in the Vicinity
Key Monastery

Spit Valley has its own whole storehouse of monasteries owing to its rich religious cultural heritage influenced by Tibetan Buddhism. The Key Monastery is nestled in the midst of snow-covered mountains and it’s quite an unworldly breathtaking sight. History enthusiasts will be enthralled by the traditional architecture and mural paintings displayed inside the monastery, along with ancient documents and books that have been preserved over such a long period of time.

Chandratal

At 4300 m above sea level, the Chandratal lake sits untouched from the ravages of industrialization and other man-made hazards. The water here is so clear that one can see the reflection of the picturesque landscape and mountains surrounding the lake, almost as if it were a painting. Other than blessing the tourists with these majestic views, it’s also one of the most popular destinations in Spiti for trekkers.

Pin Valley National Park

Rare species like snow leopards and the Ibex can be found living here.

Tabo Monastery

This monastery located in Tabo Valley is a treasure ground for Buddhist culture and its ancient traditions. The bright yet warm architecture of this Gompa stands in stark contrast with the overarching spectacle of the barren cold desert. Also known as “Ajanta of The Himalayas”, this monastery isn’t perched on any hilltop like most other structures of this type, it’s rather found at the bottom of Tabp Valley. Due to its immaculate preservation after all this time, the Archeological Survey of India has proclaimed it to be the “National historic treasure” of India.

Suraj Tal

Encompassing the Baralacha pass and originating from Bhaga tributary of Chandrabhaga river, Suraj Tal is the 21st highest lake in the world and 3rd highest in India. It’s enclosed between mountains surrounding it on all sides, so its panoramic vision appeases the mind. Moreover, it has a religious connection too as locals consider it to be the lake of Sun God. Adventure enthusiasts delight in undertaking trekking expeditions from here.

Dhankar Monastery

This popular tourist attraction stands on a cliff at an altitude of 12,774 ft. It was constructed thousand years ago and one can spot the confluence of the Spiti and Pin rivers. The monastery also has a micro museum and the statue of Dhayan Buddha. You can also visit the Dhankar lake by hiking for a 2 km distance from the monastery.

Trilokinath Temple

At a distance of 9 km from Udaipur village, the standing Trilokinath temple serves as a significant pilgrimage site for both Hindus and Buddhists. With its ancient name as Tunda Vihar, the temple structure is painted in all white and is nestled at a height of 2760 m. One can spot the Tibetan prayer flags blowing in the wind from a distance.

Keylong Market

If you’ve been wanting to purchase woolen clothes made from real Pashmina and Angora yarn, this market will leave you awestruck. Huge varieties of mufflers, shawls etc can be found here at affordable prices. Some other unique mementos that can be bought from here include local handicrafts and jewelry, ceramic utensils, and more.

Tayul Monastery

This Buddhist monastery is situated in the Bhaga Valley and holds Padma Sambhava’s biggest statue. About a hundred million prayer wheels can ve found inside the Gompa and spinning them is sought to bring some spiritual enlightenment and have a calming effect on the worshippers.

Best Activities You Can Enjoy in the Vicinity

The cold desert valley of Spiti has quite a few captivating activities under its hood. Once you unravel this list of exciting adventures, you’ll be left asking for more.

Trekking

Dhankar lake is situated on a cliff at an altitude of 4270 m. Trekking to this location requires a lot of endurance and strength but when completed, it powers you up with rejuvenating energy. Once you start from Dhankar village, it will take you about an hour to complete the trek to the lake and you’ll be blessed with a view you’ve never laid your eyes upon before in your life.

River Rafting

The waters that provide ideal conditions for this adventure sport are Spiti and Pin rivers. Rafting through the swift water currents while being surrounded by snow-capped mountains on all sides, and the proximity with the beauty of nature will transport you to a serene and safe haven. Monsoon creates tricky and dangerous obstacles for this sport so it’s best to steer clear of these waters during the season.

Yak Safari

This is one of the local activities that will make you feel one with the place and the people there. Most families in the villages of Spiti have a yak as yak rearing is actually a means of livelihood for the people. So, you can find them in huge abundance in the valley. Going on yak safari through the rugged mountainous terrain of Spiti to explore the landscape is one recreational activity that should definitely be on your list.

Shopping For Souvenirs

The main Kaza market is heaven for anyone who loves shopping. You will be able to find traditional souvenirs to remind you of your Spiti Valley trip when you go back to your chaotic and bustling daily routine. Handcrafted paraphernalia of Buddhist and Tibetan cultures are commonly available throughout the entire market.

Endless loads of collector’s items will make up for your mementos of this trip. Local shops’ supply of mouth-watering sweets will keep you from leaving.

Camping

Chandratal lake is one of the wonders of Spiti Valley that tourists never miss a chance to visit. Residing in the midst of the peaceful Himalayas, the lake owes its name to its crescent moon-like shape. The calming aura exuded by the entire landscape of the area guarantees a refreshing camping experience.

Visiting the 500-Year-Old Mummy

History enthusiasts marvel at the sight harbored by Giu village. The 500-year-old remains of a meditating lama sit at a place near Gompa. After much research, the mummy was found to be more than half a century old.

Hunt For Fossils

Langza and Hikkim grant your wish of an endless archeological expedition as these villages house fossils in ample amounts. These will act as yet another souvenir for you to reminisce the memories of this trip. So many of them can be found lying around in the area within a few minutes itself.
