Dubai Tourism

Dubai is one of the most popular Emirati cities in the world. Over the last few years, the city has earned quite a reputation for itself and is one of the glistening and affluent establishments on a global scale. Due to this reason, it has also earned its nickname as the Gulf Tiger, owing to its rapidly booming economy which has also been supported by the steady rise in tourism.

The famous Barbadian-American DJ has succinctly described the city as “the hot country with a lot of money”.

The futuristic architectural designs of structures and buildings that exist in Dubai are completely in contrast with the simple and embracing population that lives there.
Its often compared with the aura of Hong Kong or Las Vegas individually. However, after landing here, one can have a first-hand experience of how it probably is a modern fusion of characteristics of both these cities, but in its own unique manner.

Popular culture and media project a very bombastic and thriving image of the city, which isn’t wrong, but there’s a lot more to uncover under the layers of the shining Emirates. The city also has its own historical background and cultural and religious ethos which can’t be ignored.

This is why we’ve curated a holistic account of the city, covering most of its aspects, dos, and donts, that you’ll have to jot down before flying off to the Emirates.

Here’s all that you need to know about the Gulf Tiger - Dubai.

How to Reach

By Air



The best and most comfortable way of traveling to Dubai is by flying there. There are a handful of airports connecting flights to Dubai. First is Dubai International Airport. Then at a distance of 32 km from Dubai in Jebel Ali is Al Maktoum International Airport. Abu Dhabi International Airport is yet another option. Lastly, Sharjah International Airport in Sharjah.

If you’re traveling from India, then flights like Fly Dubai, Air India, Spice Jet, Emirates should be your go-to preferences.

Contrarily, for domestic air travels Air Arabia and Fly Dubai are the flights you should consider.

By Train



Emirates Express operates for Emirate nations. You can board this train to reach Dubai if you’re traveling within UAE.

By Road



Bus service is a rather cheap alternative as opposed to taxis or cabs.
Covering distance domestically via the road is a good idea. However, traveling straight from India to Dubai by bus is not a comforting image.

The road journey between New Delhi and Dubai amounts to a duration of around 60 hours, and from Mumbai, it’s even more.

Network Connectivity Status

Two top network operators work in Dubai - Du (Dubai) and Etisalat (Abu Dhabi).
The majority of the population uses pre-paid network plans. You can customize your plan as per your budget. The international roaming plans are also quite affordable so you can easily continue contacting your family members.

You merely require your passport and a mode of payment to acquire your pre-paid SIM card in Dubai. This can even be done at the airport itself.
Recent surveys have unlocked an amazing milestone that reveals that 4G can be accessed nearly at all the locations of the UAE. Additionally, Etisalat is yet victorious and has proved to show a faster download and upload speed than Du.

Top Places to Eat

Nobu



Managed by the Japanese celebrity chef Nobuyuki Matsuhisa, Nobu has gained an elite stature among the many luxury dine-in experiences. It was co-founded by Hollywood maestro Robert De Niro alongside Nobuyuki. There’s a good chance that you might even catch a glimpse of a famous public figure while dining here, and its credit is due to the quality food served at one’s table with panache.

Amaseena



An extension of the Ritz Carlton Jumeirah Hotel, Amaseena visualizes an authentic Arabic setting with traditional dishes acting as the cherry on top. The outdoor sitting arrangement seeks to transport you to an Arabic desert lookalike.

Bistrot Bagatelle



This restaurant is commonly renowned as a late-night dine-in French eatery. The place satiates one’s desire to devour delicious non-vegetarian as well as vegetarian options. Even after having your main course, you won’t be able to bring yourself to exit the venue as cinnamon rolls or lemon cheesecakes will entice you with their sugary charm. Moreover, the DJ playing in the background will keep you thoroughly entertained.

Masti



La Mer’s Masti is an Indian restaurant helmed and its location beside the beach adds to the whole foodie experience which boasts of fusion experimentations. The restaurant has bagged several awards and been acclaimed as the Best Indian Restaurant. Its recipes, as helmed by culinary director Prashant Chipkar, have redefined traditional Indian cuisine by introducing a modern twist to it.

Arabian Tea House Cafe



A popular hotspot of Arabic and Emirati food located in Al Fahidi Street, Arabian Tea House Cafe doesn’t simply serve these delicacies but also emulates the homely ambiance of Arabian culture. The cafe boasts of an extensive tea collection originating from various nations like India, Canada, Australia, the Czech Republic, and China.

Top Dishes You Must Taste

Shawarma



Your vacation can’t be over without trying the ultimate street food of Dubai. This juicy roll of grilled meat and other vegetable additions is a relatively healthier option on the street that doesn’t take a toll on your wallet either. The best part is how filling it really is that it could even serve as a meal considering it's a combination of all sorts of foods - bread, vegetables, meat, etc.

Luqaimat



Usually relished during the celebrations of Ramadan, Luqaimat is a fried sugary ball that reminds one of gulab jamun. Servings of these Arabic dumplings are accompanied by honey or maple syrup.

Manousheh



Having earned its name as Dubai’s pizza, Manousheh is a great Lebanese breakfast meal that offers a great variety of toppings. You can top the flatbread with anything of your choice ranging from eggs, greek yogurt, meat, or even hazelnut Nutella chocolate for that matter. You just can’t go wrong with this dish because it leaves so much room for experimentation.

Iranian Sangak



The healthy baked whole wheat Iranian flatbread has earned quite a name for itself in Dubai. It’s generally enjoyed with a serving of yogurt and feta cheese. However, its quality is the best right after it's taken off the oven - crispy outside and soft inside.

Al Harees



This one’s mostly savored during festivities so it takes on a more traditional role in the list of our must-have dishes in Dubai. It’s one of the most popular and best dishes of Dubai that has also proved to bring people together due to their shared mutual love for it.

Local Culture and People

As mentioned earlier the Emirati culture hinges on certain conventions that some people may not be familiar with. As far as the locals are concerned, they’re very warm-hearted and welcoming people, and this is also reflected in the way they greet friends. Their salutations are usually supported by warm mentions to God and embracing one another.

However, one shouldn’t engage on such terms with women, unless they initiate the greeting in a similar manner on their own, even then hugs and other physically intimate gestures should be avoided. Moreover, even as per basic etiquette, you shouldn’t snap any pictures of Emirati women. Such actions won’t be looked at in a positive light.

The major festival celebration is during the month of Ramadan when Muslims tend to fast on a daily basis. As a visitor during this month, you shouldn’t be drinking, smoking, chewing gum, or eating out in public. These regulations have also been sanctioned by the government, so you need to be mindful of them.

Another thing to be kept in mind is for you to dress up moderately and foreigners visiting the Emirati region shouldn’t be openly adorning the native national dress except for when on desert safaris or any other occasion that offers them to do so.

Best Time to Visit

The time span between the months of November to April serves as the ideal window for you to travel overseas to Dubai. The winter season that falls in between this time frame offers pleasant conditions for you to enjoy your stay instead of suffering through the adverse hot extremes of the place. Moreover, since there’s barely any rainfall throughout the year, the humidity also tends to stay in control and not go overboard most of the time. The sea temperature is warm enough as well for you to partake in any kind of water sports or activities.

Essentials to Pack For the Trip

A lot of consideration needs to be put in while packing your essentials for Dubai. There are a lot of restrictions and regulations that need to be adhered to while doing so.

First and foremost importance needs to be given to your important documents without which international travelling won’t even be possible. Passports, visas, travel insurance. Identity proofs like your driver’s license or voter ID and some photocopies of all these documents.

Other basic requirements that are universal to any and every vacation are electronic gadgets like smartphones, chargers, power banks, laptops, headphones, earphones, etc.

When it comes to clothing, public locations like malls, restaurants, hotels, and markets demand you to wear something conventional that doesn't bare your shoulders and knees. Religious monuments or buildings have even stricter policies. You must be fully covered before entering such places. At the beach, you can relax a bit and choose your attire as per your comfort.
However, you must remember to follow the former guidelines before heading out.

Owing to the hot conditions of Dubai, you may require sunscreen, caps, or hats to protect yourself from the sun’s severity.

Basic medicines and first aid kits are another necessity that you will need for the trip.

Top Places to Explore in the Vicinity

La Mer



It’s a beachfront location in Dubai around the Jumeirah neighborhood. The avant-garde aesthetic of this development plan packs in a chain of cafes, shops, and other activities. One can rent a cabana facing the beach and make the most of their staycation by having a clast by the beach.

Dubai Marina



Typically a residential district, Dubai Marina is one of the most prosperous locales in Dubai. The man-made marina stretches to a distance of 3 km, with high-end eating outlets, malls, cafes and markets dot this stretch.

Bur Dubai Abra Dock



This historic station is lined up with Abra, the traditional boat made of wood, for the people to enjoy a boat ride up to Gold Souk. During this ferry, tourists will come across several other attractions as well like various art galleries and Textile Souk.

Palm Islands



Palm Jumeirah, Palm Jebel Ali, and Deira Island make up a group of three artificial islands that are collectively known as the Palm Islands. This project is labeled as the largest manmade group of islands, which has also led it to bag the status of the supposed “the eighth wonder of the world”. Out of the three islands, only Palm Jumeirah is open to the public, which is home to the famous affluent hotel, Burj Al Arab.

Dubai Creek



Unlike quite a few manmade establishments and mega projects in Dubai, this one is a natural saltwater creek that comes between Bur Dubai and Deira. Also called Khor Dubai, the creek allows visitors to go on a ride in an abra and explore the old site of the city on both sides of it.

Riverland Dubai



Situated on the Sheikh Zayed Road, Riverland Dubai is a shopping complex whose visual aesthetics are a reminder of Indian, mid-century American, and French architecture. People of all ages are welcome here as it serves as a perfect destination for you to hangout with your loved ones.

It stands beside a 1 km long manmade river. Some of these attractions include various theme parks like Bollywood Parks and Legoland. Other than that, the place also hosts many live performances while serving cuisines from all over the world.

Bastakiya Quarter



The foundation of this residential district dates back to the 1890s. While strolling through this locality, you will be transported to the olden days of Dubai when manmade industrial marvels were merely a dream. The place is also known as the Al Fahidi Neighbourhood and it withholds several art galleries. The few eateries functioning here will unlock the memoirs of the traditional Emirati meal.

Souk Madinat Jumeirah



This retail complex bridges the gap between the old heritage and the modern lifestyle of Dubai.
The bazaar holds plenty of insignia that reminds one of the old Arabian market’s heritage.
Additionally, you can also head out for a boat ride in the wooden abra.

Top Attractions in the Vicinity

Burj Khalifa



It comes as no surprise that Burj Khalifa is the most famous attraction of Dubai that lures a huge number of tourists to the city. As the tallest building in the world, it’s a standing landmark that boasts of the progressive and modern developments that have come to transform Dubai’s map. The 160 storeyed building is a hotspot for extravagant and opulent dining and entertainment experiences.

Ferrari World



Abu Dhabi’s Yas Island offers you a racing opportunity to witness the very first Ferrari-themed amusement park museum. The adrenaline junkie in you will be pumped to be at a place like this where you can first-hand experience the thrill of the highest loop ride, the world’s fastest rollercoaster, and numerous other such rides.

Burj Al Arab Jumeira



Being the third tallest hotel in the world, this luxurious and lavish accommodation has now become Dubai’s popular reflection. It’s also one of the most expensive hotels around the globe and its foundation being laid down on an artificially manufactured island adds to its individuality.
Guests staying here have the privilege to be a part of top-tier experiences like dining at Michelin star restaurants, short trips in Rolls Royce and helicopters, etc.

Bollywood Parks



If you’re a fanatic of Bollywood flicks who can’t get enough of this glamour world, you must visit the world’s first Bollywood theme park in Dubai. The theme park packs in a fusion of various entertainments ranging from stunt performances, 4D thrill rides, musicals, Indian street food and so much more.

The Miracle Garden



2013’s Valentine’s Day marked the inaugural date of the world’s largest natural flower garden in the district of Dubailand. Hundreds of million plant varieties are housed here. In addition to that, the venue often hosts music and dance events for the entertainment of the visitors. The radiant, multi-colored flower installations will leave you mesmerized as if spellbound.

Dubai Frame



This colossal vision of a picture frame at a height of 150 m is one of the tallest structures and the largest frame in the world. Zabeel Park’s Dubai Frame offers views of both the old and new Dubai. Walking through the glass pathway of the structure is like walking through a gateway that connects the past and the future of Dubai.

Jumeirah Mosque



With its motto “Open Doors. Open Minds”, Jumeirah Mosque welcomes everyone regardless of what their faith is. The White-stoned architecture first opened up to worshippers in 1979. Tours are conducted here for anyone who wishes to get to know more about the place and its religious traditions.

Grand Mosque



Dubai’s cultural heritage finds itself embodied in this Islamic architecture. With the Islamic inscriptions welcoming you and the city’s tallest minaret awaiting, the mosque can hold a large gathering up consisting of 1200 worshippers. Residing in the old city, the building was constructed originally in 1900 but underwent multiple renovations to look like what it does today.

The Green Planet



A man-made biodome with 3000 varieties of plants, the Green Planet also houses a lot of animals like squirrel monkeys, Cuban Amazon parrots, piranhas, etc. This indoor tropical rainforest is the gateway to a huge load of knowledge about the need to preserve the natural exotic flora and fauna that co-exist with us, thereby making it a great picnic spot for everyone regardless of one’s age.

Dubai Museum



Located in Al Fahidi Fort, Dubai Museum has preserved Dubai’s history since 1800, which also makes it the city’s oldest establishment. The old city lifestyle is exhibited in various ways in the Museum. Videos and film footage display quite a few highlights of the same. The place reflects a lived trajectory of the city starting from its early reliance on fishing to its current enormous status as one of the richest cities in the world.

Must-Do Activities

Sky Diving (Palm Jumeirah)



This one-of-a-kind free-falling experience is one of the best attractions of Dubai. “Skydive Dubai” is the best operator in the city that allows tourists to dive straight into the beauteous skyline that offers a view of Burj Al Arab and the Palm Jumeirah island, etc.

Ride on the Largest Ferris Wheel (Ain Dubai)



Located on Bluewaters Island, Ain Dubai is the tallest Ferris wheel in the world. Overlooking Dubai Marina, Ain Dubai displays the golden city’s skyline as you observe it all from 250 m in the air. The picturesque views from such a height are undoubtedly breathtaking, to which the city’s night glory only adds to the twinkling delight.

Indoor Skiing (Ski Dubai)



Covering the area of 22,500 sq m, Ski Dubai is an indoor ski resort and has even bagged accolades for the same in 2016 and 2017. Nestled in one of the largest malls in the world - Mall of the Emirates, the ski resort maintains extreme temperatures to provide the visitors with an experience as close to the authentic skiing landscape as possible.

Hot Air Ballooning



There are many ways to soar above a city, hot air balloons being one of those mediums. You can enjoy this ride with your loved ones and enjoy the views of the expansive landscape of the Gulf Tiger while witnessing a mellow sunset.

Lake Riding (Dubai Fountain)



Across the 900 ft expanse of the Dubai Fountain Lake, this ride takes you through a music and light performance set in water on an abra boat.

Meet Dolphins (Dubai Dolphinarium)



One of the most happening indoor attractions of Dubai is Dubai Dolphinarium. Here you can watch dolphins and seals perform to their heart’s content. During the show, you can bear witness to their upbeat side while watching them dance, jump and perform stunts right in front of your eyes.
